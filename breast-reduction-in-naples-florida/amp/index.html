How is breast reduction surgery performed?

One of the most important moments of the operation is the need to preserve the blood supply of the nipple-areolar complex, which moves to a higher position. Depending on the amount of excess tissue needed to be removed and, accordingly, which skin-fat flap the blood supply functions will be preserved, the surgical incisions can be made:

On the circumference of the areola – this option of breast reduction is used when removing a small part of the breast tissue.

Gynecomastia – Male Breast Reduction With Doctor Kent V. Hasen, MD, Naples Plastic Surgeon

Around the areola and vertically downward, perpendicular to the pectoral fold – this incision, which has the form of a tennis racket, is optimal for excising medium volumes.

Circling along the areola border, down the vertical line and horizontally in the submammary fold – the “anchor” type of the incision allows to remove up to 2 lb 3 oz. of excess tissue.

Before the operation, a medical examination is prescribed, and recommendations for preparation for mammoplasty are given. Intervention occurs under general anesthesia.

The surgeon makes the marking of the breast, excludes excess skin flaps, then the underlying fat and glandular tissues, moves the nipple-areolar complex, keeping the vessels, nerves and milk ducts.

After that, the tissue is sewn, tightens the skin and imposes cosmetic sutures. If the breast is too large (gigantomastia), the nipple-areolar complex can be moved in a free order or even removed with subsequent reconstruction from other tissues.

Recovery after reduction mammoplasty

The first day (and if necessary, two days) after surgery a woman is in the hospital, and then continues to recover at home with a periodic visit to the doctor. The surgeon will remove the stitches on the 10th-12th day. About one and a half months, you can not remove compression underwear, do physical work, play sports.

Until the completion of the recovery period, pregnancy is categorically contraindicated. The final result of reduction mammoplasty is estimated after 5-6 months.

Contraindications to breast reduction surgery in Naples, FL

  • Age to 18 years.
  • Pregnancy, the period of breastfeeding + 1 year after cessation of lactation.
  • Poor blood coagulation and other hematologic problems.
  • Acute periods of chronic diseases.
  • Diabetes.
  • Active oncology.
  • Neoplasms in the area of mammary glands.
  • Skin inflammation in the operational area.
