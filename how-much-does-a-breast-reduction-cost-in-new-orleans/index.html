In fact, breast reduction in New Orleans is no less popular surgery. Another thing is that unlike mastopexy (breast lifts) or breast augmentation, breast reduction is, firstly, mostly necessary medical procedure, and is carried out for medical reasons, and, secondly, it has many side effects.

In this case, the most complex operation to increase and correct the breast requires a maximum of 3 hours. In addition, compared with cosmetic surgery, the percentage of complications is very high, and requiring further surgical intervention.

With a small change in the volume of the breast, reduction, which is the removal of excess soft tissue and partial elimination of the lobules of the mammary glands, is not terrible.

But we must take into account, that the volume of milk in a nursing mother has little correlation with the size of the breast, but depends on the number of lobules. If the difference in the initial and final volumes is great, and it will not be possible to do without excision of adipose tissue, then with the possible next pregnancy with breastfeeding, problems can arise – if at all possible.

Depending on the volume of tissues from which you want to get rid of, different methods of performing the operation are used, because with reduction mammoplasty in New Orleans, you can remove both a half 1 lb and 4 lb – 6 lb of excess tissue in the gland. And if in the first case, an insignificant incision is made for the operation in an inconspicuous submammary (under the breast) skin fold, the removal of a large volume of fat and glandular tissues will have to be combined with correction of excess skin and excision of stretch marks, and at the same time it will be necessary to supplement the restoration of the “exterior” mastopexy procedure and plastic surgery of nipples. As a result, it is difficult to even guess how long the operation can take.

This will be needed not only to again give the breast a natural appearance (or to return it to normal, if the breast reduction was required after the birth of the child).

This is necessary, first of all, so that there are no problems with the ducts, otherwise difficulties with future breastfeeding are possible. And secondly, if the breast is large and sagging, the areola and nipple can move down a considerable distance, you need to predict where they will be located on the mammary glands after the operation.
