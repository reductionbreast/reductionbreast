Breast Reduction In Omaha Nebraska

Breast reduction surgery with the second name “reduction mammoplasty” is used to remove excess soft tissue of the breast.

You can perform a breast reduction operation to eliminate physical discomfort or to achieve a breast size that is in harmony with other body proportions.

Breast reduction surgery in Omaha is designed for women who are concerned about the following problems associated with too large a breast size:

– Chronic pain in the back, neck and shoulders;
– Chronic rash or irritation of the skin under the breast;
– Deep grooves on the shoulders from the pressure of the strap of the bra;
– Physical activity is difficult;
– Strong emotional experiences and low self-esteem associated with large breasts;
– Complexity of selection of a bra and clothes;
– Problems with sleep.

You can do reduction mammoplasty, the price of which in our clinic is affordable and democratic, at any age. In some cases (if there is evidence), breast reduction is performed during adolescence. However, it is better to wait until the full development of the mammary glands.

If you do not have a baby yet, surgery can be postponed, as changes in breast tissue during pregnancy and after breastfeeding can reduce the effect of surgery.

In addition, breastfeeding after some kinds of reducing mammoplasty can be difficult. But more often it is due to the lack of proper instruction, and not to the operation itself.

Cost of operation

Reduction mammoplasty, the price of which is formed of the time spent on the intervention, the number of consumables, technical complexity of the operation, vary in cost in different countries. So in the capital clinics the procedure will cost a little more than in other cities. Possible risks and complications

Breast Reduction Treatment With Dr. Nagi T. Ayoub

Breast reduction in Omaha has the same risks as any other operation – infection in the postoperative wound, bleeding and a side reaction to anesthesia. Other possible risks include:

  • Scarring;
  • Loss of sensation in the nipples and skin around the nipples (areola);
  • Difficulty or inability to breastfeed;
  • Differences in size, shape and symmetry (asymmetry) after surgical changes in the right and left mammary glands, which may require further surgical intervention to improve the appearance.

The risk of poor wound healing increases with the amount of removed breast tissue. Some facts show that women with a higher body mass index and obesity are at greater risk of complications after a breast reduction operation.

If in a short time after the reduction mammoplasty any complications develop, the measures for their correction will also be included in the cost and the total price for the plastic of the mammary glands will be higher.

  • Submit a list of laboratory tests;
  • Make a mammogram;
  • Quit smoking for a certain period of time before and after surgery to reduce the risk of complications;
  • Avoid taking aspirin, herbal supplements and anti-inflammatory drugs before surgery.
  • Preliminary examinations (tests, mammography) also increase the total cost of the operation and preparation for it.

Ask your surgeon if you will be able to leave the clinic on the day of surgery, or you will have to spend the night in the clinic. The length of stay in the clinic also affects the price of reduction mammoplasty.

Usually, breast reduction surgery is performed under general anesthesia, either in a hospital or in an outpatient.

Surgery and postoperative period

The methods used to reduce breast size may vary. The price of breast reduction depends on the method chosen.

Typically, the surgeon makes an incision around the areola and below the breast. Excess tissue of the breast, skin and fat is removed to reduce the total volume of the breast.

Bust Reduction Surgery Reshapes The Breasts To Make Them Smaller, Lighter And Firmer

In most cases, the nipple-areola complex is sought to keep connected to the mammary gland.

If the breast is very large and sagging, it may be necessary to separate the nipple and areola with their subsequent suturing back to a higher position in the skin flap.

After operation

After the intervention, the wounds can be drained by tubes to remove excess blood or fluid, and be covered with bandages. The doctor prescribes painkillers and antibiotics to reduce the risk of infection. Your surgeon can recommend an elastic compression bra for breast protection.

During healing, within two to four weeks, you need to limit physical activity. Also, the surgeon can recommend not wearing bras on bones within a month after the operation.

The cost of consumables, medicines and special underwear also increases the overall cost of reduction mammoplasty.
