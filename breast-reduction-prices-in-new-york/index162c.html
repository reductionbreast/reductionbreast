Breast Reduction Prices In New York

Breast reduction surgery (reduction mammoplasty) in New York City is designed to remove tissue and skin from the breast to change its shape and reduce the size.

It is also used to reduce the dark skin around the nipple. The surgeon first makes one or more incisions to remove tissue and skin from the chest.

After removing excess tissue and skin, the seam is stitched. Sometimes it is necessary to remove the nipple and the peripapillary circle in order to fix them in a new place.

This can lead to a loss of sensitivity of the nipple as it is cut from the nerves of attached thereto. Breast reduction surgery is performed in a hospital or surgical center, using general anesthesia.

The operation usually takes 3 to 5 hours. You do not always need to stay in hospital overnight.

Breast Hypertrophy Can Alter Your Lifestyle

Breast lift (mastopexy) is similar to breast reduction, except in cases where only the skin is removed during breast lift.

Breast lift can lift the sagging or drooping breasts, which is a common problem in the case of large breasts and can also raise the nipple and peripapillary circle.

Excess skin below the breast and in the area around the peripapillary circle is removed to lift the breasts. The remaining skin connects, which strengthens and lifts the breast.

What to expect after the operation?

Immediately after the operation, gauze is applied to the incision site, the breast is bandaged with an elastic bandage or supported by a special surgical bra.

In some cases, each breast may have a small tube to facilitate the outflow of blood and fluid throughout the first few days. Stitches can be removed after 1 – 2 weeks.

Many women may have chest pain during the first few days after surgery, and then feel a slight discomfort for a week or longer. Medications can help in overcoming pain.

Puffiness and bruising can remain for several weeks. Wearing a surgical bra 24 hours a day can help reduce puffiness and supports the breast during healing.

Big Breasts May Make You Feel Self-conscious

You will be able to return to work and social activity in a few weeks, if this does not mean lifting weights or strong physical exertion.

You may need to avoid exercise for 3-4 weeks or more. It is necessary to wear a bra that supports the breast well, for example, athletic or athletic.

After the operation to reduce the chest, you will have visible scars. They will be in those places that are hidden by a bra or swimsuit.

Over time, the scars will become less noticeable, but will not disappear completely.

Reasons

Breast reduction surgery is performed to change the size, weight, firmness and shape of the breast. You can perform a breast reduction surgery to:

  • Feel more comfortable. A large, heavy chest can cause pain in the back and neck, irritation of the skin, and problems with posture. Constant tension of the large breast can contribute to the fact that bra straps leave painful traces on the woman’s shoulders.
  • Breast reduction surgery can get rid of these problems in most cases.
  • Reduce the restrictions caused by large, heavy breasts when doing sports or other activities. Some physical exercises can cause pain or embarrassment in women with large breasts.
  • Change your appearance. Big breasts, especially if it is disproportionately large, compared to your height and weight, can embarrass you.
  • Teenagers and young women with large breasts may feel particularly awkward when wearing swimsuit or other clothing because of undesirable attention being attracted by the big breasts. It can also be difficult to find clothes that fit well.

Is it effective?

Women who have undergone breast reduction surgery are often very pleased with the results. The operation makes the breast less, more firm and light, and also more proportional.

This usually reduces the physical discomfort and pain caused by the large breasts. The results of breast reduction surgery are considered permanent. But the breast can increase or change its shape as a result of pregnancy, losing weight or excess weight.

Risks

The most common breast reduction risks are:

Scars. Breast reduction surgery often leaves visible scars on the breast, but how strong will the scars depends on the person and the type of incision. Although the scars are first red and swollen, they usually, with time, decrease. But the scars can be very noticeable in some women and after the operation. Fortunately, the incision is usually limited to the site of the breast, and can be covered with a bra.
Unevenly located nipples, or breasts of different size or shape.
Loss of sensation of the nipple or breast. This is often a temporary phenomenon lasting several months, but in some women it lasts longer or becomes permanent. Since, some women with large breasts before surgery sensitivity was small, they do not care.
Inability to breastfeed after surgery. Some women can breastfeed, depending on the type of reduction done.
Less often during the operation, the blood supply to the breast may be damaged. This can delay the healing process. The loss of part or all of the nipple and peripapillary circle can also occur, but this happens rarely.

Other risks of surgery include:

  • Severe bleeding during surgery.
  • Infection.
  • The reaction to the anesthesia.
  • Blood clots large veins that can get to the heart or the lungs (pulmonary embolism). This happens rarely.
    These risks can be serious, or even life-threatening, but rarely occur.

Remember that breast reduction can complicate breastfeeding in the future, or make it impossible, although some women retain the ability to breastfeed after a breast reduction operation.

If you want to reduce breasts, contact your insurance company. Some insurance companies cover some or all of the cost of breast reduction, if it is carried out in order to reduce back pain, skin problems, or other health problems caused by large or heavy breasts.

They usually do not cover the cost of a breast reduction operation, if it is performed solely for the purpose of changing the shape of the breast, since this is not considered medically necessary.
