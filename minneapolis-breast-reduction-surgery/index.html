Minneapolis Breast Reduction Surgery

Breast reduction in Minneapolis is another important method of surgical correction, pursuing, in addition to the aesthetic goal, also a therapeutic one.

Hypertrophy of the mammary glands, which is very common in elderly women and often in young patients, is a serious problem leading to increased stress on the spine and hypertension of muscles, as well as interfering with active lifestyles and making it difficult to diagnose pathologies.

Plastic operations for breast reduction in Minneapolis are considered the most complicated among the types of mammoplasty.

First of all, because of the difficulties associated with maintaining normal blood circulation.

In fact, breast reduction involves removing excess fatty tissue and excess skin, that is, combining liposuction and a breast lift.

Determination of indications and preparation for surgery

In determining the appropriateness of carrying out and choosing the type of plastic surgery for breast reduction, consider:

  • Causes of hypertrophy;
  • The degree of breast enlargement;
  • Presence of mastopexy;

The cause of hypertrophy of the mammary glands, along with a genetic predisposition, may be a violation of the hormonal balance, so before the operation it is necessary to identify the cause and correct hormonal disorders.

Otherwise, the breast will increase and after the operation.

Hypertrophy of the mammary glands during pregnancy and breast-feeding often is reversible. So talking about carrying out reduction mammoplasty is worth only half a year after full completion of lactation.

There are generally accepted objective criteria for the degree of hypertrophy of the mammary glands: Normally the volume of one breast should not exceed 300 cm3. However, in determining the indications for surgery, not only the absolute size of the gland is taken into account, but also its ratio with the growth of the patient and the volume of the thorax proper.

Methods of plastic surgery and postoperative scars

The success of the operation is largely determined by the right choice of technique, so the main task of a plastic surgeon is to choose which parts of the skin to remove.

With minor correction, as a rule, a technique is selected with preservation of upper-lower feeding flaps. In this case, the recovery period after breast reduction is small, and the scars are barely noticeable and are located only around the nipple area. Removal of lateral, upper-lateral and lower flaps is used when larger scale correction is needed.

These types of interventions are more difficult, and therefore require more time for postoperative recovery. The postoperative scar in this case surrounds the areola and descends in the form of an inverted “T”. In any case, postoperative scars are arranged in such a way that they manage to hide under clothing and swimsuit.

Aesthetics and functionality of the postoperative scar are determined not only by surgical techniques. Much depends on the patient’s compliance with medical recommendations in the postoperative period and the individual characteristics of connective tissue.

If there is a tendency to form hypertrophic scars, additional preventive measures are included.

Reduction mammoplasty is difficult to call a simple operation, however, in case of unambiguous indications, it is undoubtedly worthwhile to decide on it. After all, the consequences in the form of not found out in time diseases, violations of posture and osteochondrosis of the cervicothoracic can be much more serious.

Recovery and breastfeeding

Breast Reduction With Doctor Michael C. Fasching, MD, Minneapolis Plastic Surgeon Before And After

To significantly facilitate recovery after a breast reduction surgery in Minneapolis will help to comply with all the prescriptions of the surgeon, as well as wearing postoperative body and bras of the ComfortWear line (Marena, USA). Their eco-friendly material and special design reduces the load on the skin and muscle tissue, providing, among other things, antimicrobial action.

The main feature of breast reduction surgery is the impossibility of normal lactation in the future. Plastic surgeons are trying to choose the most gentle technique operation, but to assert unequivocally that during the reduction mammoplasty not was broken system of milk ducts, it is impossible.

The ability of the gland to lactation is completely preserved, but due to a change in the anatomy of the excretory ducts, the likelihood of lactostasis and inflammation of the gland increases. Therefore, the need for breast-feeding should be eliminated before the intervention.

The operation is not recommended for patients, who have not yet given birth and because the breasts may increase again during pregnancy and breast-feeding, which will require an additional correction.
