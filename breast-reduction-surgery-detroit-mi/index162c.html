Breast Reduction Surgery In Detroit MI

Breast reduction, or reduction mammoplasty in Detroit, MI is required in cases where there is an excess amount of breast tissue.

As a rule, reduction mammoplasty is combined with a breast lift – mastopexy.

Indications for breast reduction surgery:

– Changes in the volume of mammary glands and their ptosis after pregnancy and breastfeeding
– Certain professional requirements for body contours
– Post-traumatic injuries of mammary glands
– Condition after mastectomy (removal of the breast)
– Gynecomastia and pseudo gynecomastia in men
– Violations of the symmetry of the areola of the nipple.

During surgery, excess glandular tissue and subcutaneous fatty tissue is partially removed, and new mammary glands of new size and shape are formed. In some cases, reduction mammoplasty is not only aesthetic, but also therapeutic.

Breast Reduction Mammoplasty

This is a pronounced hypertrophy of the mammary gland (gigantomastia), which accompanies various degrees of obesity and often leads to changes in posture and spine diseases.

In addition, resected excess mammary gland tissue is often changed as a result of mastopathy, and its removal is beneficial, as it reduces the risk of malignant degeneration of the breast.

Removal of breast tissue does not entail an increase in the risk of cancer in the future.

However, it should be borne in mind, that reduction surgery in Detroit and some types of mastopexy are accompanied by trauma to the mammary gland tissue and nipple-areolar complex, so the possibility of subsequent lactation may decrease.

Reduction of mammary glands in men

More often, the increase in breast glands in men (gynecomastia) is formed against the background of taking hormonal drugs (for example, antiandrogens, anabolic steroids or female sex hormones). With obesity, pseudo-gynecomastia develops.

Breast Reduction Post Surgery With Dr Andrew M. Lofman, Detroit Plastic Surgeon

Men tend to accumulate large amounts of subcutaneous fat in the thoracic glands.

A bust is formed similar to that of the female breast, while the mammary glands themselves have normal anatomical dimensions. Gynecomastia and pseudo-gynecomastia give the representatives of the stronger sex a lot of psychological difficulties – the only solution to the aesthetic problem is mastectomy.

Breast lift (mastopexy)

Inevitably, over time, under the influence of its own weight, the mammary gland will hang. Sagging breasts contributes to reduction of skin elasticity and lactation, combined with hormonal changes breast tissue.

Breast lift, or mastopexy is reduced to moving the breast to the top and removing excess skin of the breast. In those situations, where the sagging of the skin is accompanied by involuntary changes in the glandular tissue, mastopexy is supplemented by the installation of breast implants.

There are several ways of mastopexy. The surgeon’s choice is based on the initial condition of the mammary glands, breast skin and aesthetic requirements of the patient.

Areola reduction surgery

A similar type of surgical intervention is performed to correct the form of areola, size (increase or decrease in the diameter of the areola), and the location of the areola of the mammary glands.

During certain types of reduction and breast lift, surgeons can partially or completely affect the areola area.

If, with the chosen method of mammoplasty, a free transplantation of the nipple-areola complex is necessary, the patient should take into account, that breastfeeding is not possible in the future.
