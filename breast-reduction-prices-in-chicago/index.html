Many women dream about a magnificent beautiful bust. But very often those representatives of the fair sex, who by nature inherited really impressive forms, they are not happy at all.

When is breast reduction necessary?

Big breasts visually increases the weight and age of the owner, makes it rather difficult selection of underwear and clothing.

In addition, a heavy breast can provoke the appearance of pain in the back, lead to the development of osteochondrosis and scoliosis, cause migraines and sleep problems.

All these difficulties lead to the fact that the “bearers” of the lush bust think about the possibilities of reducing it.

Chest Reduction Is A Procedure In Which Excess Breast Tissue, Fat And Skin Is Removed

The methods of the reduction mammoplasty

The first thing to do is to meet with the surgeon for an initial consultation. During the examination of the patient, the doctor evaluates the shape and size of the mammary glands, determines the ratio of glandular and adipose tissue in them.

All this is necessary in order to choose the optimal strategy of the operation in each specific case.

Immediately before the operation, the doctor makes markings on the skin of the breast for future incisions. In most cases, the incisions are made along the contour of the areola and descend vertically downwards to the pectoral fold.

The doctor removes not only excess fat and excess skin, but also a part of the gland, and, if necessary, moves the nipple to another place. If the seams are applied correctly, then very soon the scarring will not be noticeable. The surgery lasts approximately 3 hours, and is performed under intravenous anesthesia.

Recovery after breast reduction

After the operation the patient remains in the hospital for another day. During the first days after surgical breast reduction in Chicago, there may be a slight soreness, which can be easily removed with analgesics.

For a month, you will have to walk in a special compression underwear, and you should also avoid high physical activity. The final form of the breast will take approximately 6 months after the operation, and the first changes will be seen in a month.

Contraindications to breast reduction

Breast reduction has the following contraindications:

  • oncological diseases;
  • diabetes;
  • Bleeding disorder;
  • Unfinished lactation after childbirth;
  • cardiovascular diseases;
  • obesity.
