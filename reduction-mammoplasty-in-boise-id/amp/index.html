Reduction Mammoplasty In Boise ID

A plastic operation that allows you to reduce the breasts and eliminate the pain in the back and neck, which often accompany women with large breasts.

There are several methods of performing breast reduction surgery. The optimal technique is chosen by the plastic surgeon during the consultation.

Regardless of the method chosen, the essence of plastic surgery is as follows: excess fat tissue and part of the mammary glands are removed, excess skin is excised, the breast of the desired size is formed.

The recovery period takes place in the hospital for 3-5 days.

Breastfeeding after breast reduction surgery

Breastfeeding is an important component of communication with a child after childbirth, which is not only physiological, but also spiritual function, which is of great importance for every woman.

Doctor Dean E. Sorensen, MD, Boise Plastic Surgeon Before And After

During pregnancy, the breast increases, the milk ducts swell, colostrum forms – the body prepares for the birth of a child.

Breast reduction surgery is best performed after childbirth, since after the operation, not every woman has the opportunity to breast-feed a child, and if this fact is extremely important for you, it is better to postpone the operation and do it after lactation.

During the breast reduction surgery, the milk ducts are cut, therefore, the colostrum can not form and will not go through them.

After breast reduction in Boise, you can have children, however, a woman should decide for herself how important it is for her to feed the baby and first contact with baby.

Any breast reduction technique is associated with the lactation process and affects the milk ducts. Feeding the child in some cases will be difficult, sometimes impossible.

If the patient is performing excision (amputation) of the breast with nipple transfer (this method is used in the case of gynecomastia or macromastia), then breastfeeding is not possible. During operation, the nipple is completely separated from the areola, and is moved to a new place, therefore, all the milk ducts are destroyed.

Doctor Dean E. Sorensen, MD, Boise Plastic Surgeon

This technique is used extremely rarely, as a rule, when other options are not available. If, however, in other techniques, the nipples remain on the cutaneous flap, the milk ducts are not completely destroyed, and breastfeeding may be possible, but difficult.

There is a technique for reducing the breast from the lower flap: in this case, almost always the possibility of breastfeeding remains.

Liposuction is considered to be the least traumatic method of breast reduction in Boise ID, in which the milk ducts are not affected, but one can not say with absolute certainty that there is no influence on lactation.

Until this is proven, the technique is performed for those women who want to retain the ability to lactate. In case of difficult lactation, a woman is prescribed special pills that stimulate this process, or the child is fed artificially.

You can never tell exactly how the reduction of the breast affects the feeding process, you can only rely on statistical data, which in your case may not work, so doctors recommend that breast reduction be done after childbirth.
