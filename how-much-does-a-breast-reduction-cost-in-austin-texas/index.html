Big breasts are the undoubted dignity and pride of a woman, it is always attracts attention.

However, it happens that the mammary glands are so heavy and bulky that they cause inconvenience, both moral and physical.

A woman who is forced to “wear” such a heavy breast experiences enormous pressure on the spine, and consequently, on the internal organs.

This leads to a gradual deformation of the bone structure and provokes the appearance of various diseases.

In addition, very large breasts can hang strongly, which looks unaesthetic.

A Bust Reduction Procedure Can Enhance The Aesthetics Of The Breast

To solve these problems, you can make a new shape and tighten the breast with reduction mammoplasty – breast reduction surgery.

Reduction mammoplasty in Austin is an operation to reduce mammary glands and improve their contours and dimensions by removing (resecting) glandular tissue.

Often the removal of excess tissue is favorable, as it is often deformed as a result of mastopathy – as a result, the risk of malignant degeneration of the mammary glands is reduced.

The removal of tissues and fat does not increase the risk of further oncological diseases.

OPERATION

Reduction mammoplasty in Austin is performed under anesthesia and lasts for one and a half to three hours. The surgeon removes the gland tissue, excess fat, moves the nipple with the areola to a new position. In the next stage, the skin flaps are reduced around the areola and a new contour is formed.

The tissue is joined by special cosmetic sutures, which are usually located around the areola and vertically down to the crease under the breast. In some cases, up to 2 kilograms of the tissue of the mammary glands and fatty tissues are removed from each breast.

The effect of reduction mammoplasty persists long enough under the condition of constant weight control. If the woman gets fat, the breast also increases.

What are the risks associated with breast reduction?

Breast reduction surgery is not one of simple operations, but, nevertheless, is associated with a low level of risk. But in the case of surgical intervention, one can never exclude possible risks completely, in some cases they can be unpredictable. Possible complications are best discussed during a personal conversation with your plastic surgeon.

As in the case of any operation, hematomas and swelling may occur. Only in very rare cases, they will have to be treated with the subsequent operation, but, as a rule, they disappear after a few days – a maximum of one week after the operation.

In rare cases, infection of the edges of the wound and violation of the healing process of the wound can occur, accompanied by a divergence of the edges of the suture, which may lead to a subsequent widening of the scars. Most often, the reason for this are factors that are discussed before the operation, for example, diabetics, smokers and patients with bleeding disorders are affected by the slow healing of wounds.

Difficult healing of the sutures, as a rule, does not affect the new form of the breast. Clearly noticeable expansion of the scar can be corrected later, by carrying out a small operation.

Also, numbness of the skin and nipples may temporarily occur in the area of the operation, which is caused by a violation of the tender nerve endings of the skin. As healing progresses, the sensitivity of the skin is fully restored.

Nevertheless, in some cases, the sensitivity in the breast and nipples areas is not restored to the full. Nipples in some rare cases may completely lose sensitivity.

Any operations leave behind more or less noticeable scars. Also, after a breast lift, there will be scars. On the nature and shape of the scars, your plastic surgeon will consult you.

Both breasts are not in perfect symmetry, neither before nor after the operation. During the operation, the maximum possible symmetry will be achieved.

Sometimes it happens that the shape and size of the breast, or the position of the nipples are not completely symmetrical. If this is clearly visible, a correction procedure may be required later.
