Breast Reduction Henderson, NV

Reduction mammoplasty in Henderson NV is a plastic surgery that is aimed at reducing the mammary glands. Mostly, it has medical, not aesthetic, indications, as often patients complain of pain in the neck, back, breathing problems and mobility restriction.

Almost always, breast reduction is combined with a breast lift, because their severity leads to ptosis.

Breast reduction surgery is performed under general anesthesia. During surgery, excess skin and part of the tissue are removed, a “new” mammary gland is formed. Surgical access to the tissue is carried out through vertical or anchor incisions.

Duration is 2-4 hours. Sutures are removed after 1-2 weeks, depending on the characteristics of the surgical procedure.

At first, the sensitivity of the breast and nipples will be reduced. Within a month, patients are advised to protect themselves from physical exertion, visiting the sauna, solarium and bath. It shows the wearing of compression underwear and medical supervision.

Preparation for breast reduction surgery

Breast reduction surgery, like any other kind of mammoplasty, is a complex surgical procedure involving a set of compulsory preparatory measures. They begin 2-3 weeks before surgery and include a number of laboratory and clinical examinations.

Laboratory clinical diagnostics

Preparation for breast reduction surgery provides for biochemical blood test for HIV, urine test. Additionally, a laboratory blood test for RW, hepatitis B and C. As part of the preparatory activities, an ECG and a mammogram is performed to identify the presence of benign and malignant formations. Based on the results of the clinical examination, a therapist’s medical report is obtained.

Consultations with a plastic surgeon

At further stages of preparation for breast reduction, there are meetings with a surgeon who will perform plastic surgery. During the examination, the general condition of the patient, the specificity of the subcutaneous fat layer, the severity of the ptosis, the skin condition are evaluated.

After examination and determining the specificity of breast hypertrophy, doctor selects the most optimal surgical technique. After that, the technique is approved with the patient, who is informed about all the features of correction, the nature of postoperative scars, general and possible complications after the procedure.

Question / Answer

When can I return to normal life?

If the patient’s work does not require lifting the weights or tension of the upper body, then a return to normal life is possible in a week after the operation. Swelling subside in 4-6 weeks, and customers can return to normal life.

How should I sleep after breast reduction?

While the scars have not healed enough, usually, about 4 weeks you should sleep lying on your back. At this time it is very important to sleep with a supporting bra.

What are the risks of breast reduction surgery?

Although the risk of complications is low enough for this type of surgery, several points can be highlighted: poor healing of scars, temporary or permanent loss of sensation of the nipple, slight asymmetry and inability of breastfeeding.
