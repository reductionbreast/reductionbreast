Techniques for breast reduction surgery

Depending on the task, plastic surgeons choose the method of reduction, that is, breast reduction. The least traumatic method is liposuction, which allows you to remove excess adipose tissue, however, you still need to do a breast lift. Every woman knows, that even just lose weight by one size, like the breast, at least a little bit, but it will sag! In that case, if it is planned to remove a small amount of breast tissue, it is possible operation carried out through an incision around the areola and creating new small areola.

Another technique consists of the incision around the areola and then a vertical incision connecting the incision below the breast. This is a more complicated operation, during which a part of the breast tissue is removed, fat tissue and then a new breast shape is created with a practically obligatory movement of the nipple and the formation of a new areola.

Complications of reduction mammoplasty

Among all breast plastic surgeries, breast reduction is the most difficult, because here the surgeon has to work not only with the skin, fatty tissue and muscles, but also with the enlarged mammary gland. Excision of the lobe of the breast requires a special approach, extensive experience and harmonious work of the entire operating team.

Complications are directly related to the complexity of operation: it may be scarring, reduced sensitivity nipple and areola after their displacement and forming, deformation or breast ptosis. Scientific studies indicate that the risk of complications is directly related to the amount of excised tissue.

Recovery after breast reduction surgery

The recovery period after breast reduction surgery is 3-4 weeks. At this time the patient can experience painful sensations in the field of incisions, discomfort. It is important at this time to wear a compression garment, correctly handle the seams, do not bend once again, sleeping on your back and eliminate physical exertion. It is compulsory to visit a plastic surgeon for examination every month during the first six months after the operation. In the future it is once every six months.
