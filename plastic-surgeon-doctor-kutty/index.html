Nose Job: bone for "columella strut"?
Bone for Columellar Strut
Apr 24, 2017
Columellar struts can be an excellent method for tip support in certain situations. Personally I rarely use bone or rib cartilage in rhinoplasty for most primary cases. When septal cartilage is properly harvested a very stout piece can be shaped into a strut that will be secure and doesn't resorb. When you see cases where tip support is lost it is almost always because the strut was too thin or not secured adequately.
Who Pays for a Brazilian Butt Lift Gone Wrong?
Figuring out who pays for BBL revision surgery
Mar 17, 2017
Keep in mind that any operation has the potential of yielding suboptimal results. Revision surgery is sometimes required, and the preop discussion between surgeon and patient should include how revisions are handled. Some surgeons will do minor "tweaks" without charge, but when a more involved correction requires use of a facility and anesthesia the patient is generally expected to pay for those outside costs.

The balance between the buttocks, torso and thighs also has to be just right to get the ideal figure. Talk to your surgeon about other considerations that might improve your results....

Do I have a fractured sternum and is it a possibility I was resucitated during surgery?
Sternal pain after breast reduction
Nov 28, 2016
It's pretty hard to fracture the sternum during a breast reduction, since the sternum and ribs are generally not even exposed during that operation. It would be extremely unusual for you to have major trauma such as chest compressions for resuscitation without being told that it happened, and without hospitalization.
Headed to surgery soon, wanted other doctors opinions: Bottom out breast (textured or smooth).
Revision breast augmentation for bottoming out
Nov 28, 2016
Your skin appears to be kind of thin, which allows implants to stretch the skin and descend due to gravity. Usually, changing to a slightly smaller implant doesn't change the weight enough to prevent it from happening again. Biologic materials are a great way to support the implant and reduce the chance of bottoming out again. You're right that textured implants are more likely to cause visible rippling, especially because your skin is thin.
Why do I keep getting capsular contracture in one side?
Recurrent Capsular Contracture
Nov 28, 2016
Capsular contracture is a result of scarring which tightens the thin connective tissue layer around the implant. Once a capsular contracture has happened, the risk is high that it will recur. There are ways to reduce the risk which are much more involved than simply opening the capsule.
