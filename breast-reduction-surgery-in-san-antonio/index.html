Breast Reduction Surgery In San Antonio

Breast reduction is sometimes called “mammoreduction” and “reduction mammoplasty.” There are many described methods, but they all boil down to the following:

– Breast reduction technique using the T-shaped incision
– Breast reduction technique by a vertical seam
– Breast reduction with periareolar incision (allows reduction only in a small amount)
– Breast reduction with internal fixation (inner bra)

2 weeks before breast reduction, smoking should be stopped in order to minimize the risk of disruption of the wound regeneration process.

The operation is performed using general anesthesia and lasts about 2 hours. The direction of the incision depends on the volume of tissue being removed.

Depending on the degree of intervention in the implementation of breast reduction, it is recommended to stay in hospital for at least one night.

Breast reduction technique using vertical incision

Breast reduction by means of a vertical incision refers to the number of techniques with a small number of scars. The scar passes around the nupple circle, and then vertically to the pectoral fold.

The incision in the transverse direction in the crease of the breast is not performed. However, due to this, the distance from the nipple to the pectoral fold and the excess of the skin located here are sufficiently large.

Therefore, the skin in this area is separated from the adipose tissue, so that the skin can be reduced better. In addition, the vertical seam is carried out in the form of a sutured suture in order to reduce the distance between the nipple and the pectoral fold.

At the same time there are small folds of the skin, which in due course should disappear due to the tightening of the skin.

The next question is the tendency to contract the skin. In the young years, this process is favorable. In addition, it is performed perfectly with a slight reduction in breast size or with a breast lift.

However, in the event that the skin is not in the optimal state, its contraction will not occur in the required volume. This also applies to cases in which breast reduction should be performed on a large scale.

In such situations, folds in the area of the vertical seam will remain noticeable for a long time. The distance from the nipple to the pectoral fold will again increase and the chest will sag.

In such cases, repeated operations are planned in advance and can lead to excellent results if they are carried out taking into account the presence of a seam in the pectoral fold.

The decision on the need for such a repeat operation is usually taken six months after breast reduction. After that, the survey data will not change significantly.

The probability of carrying out the correction operation is reduced. If the excess skin in the horizontal projection exceeds the expected size, it can be reduced by increasing the incision in the pectoral fold. As a result, a small “T-shaped incision” is formed.

L-shaped incision

The L-shaped incision is very similar to the vertical incision. In order to slightly reduce the residual surplus of the skin described above in the horizontal plane, the incision is prolonged outward in the L-shape. However, more often a “small T-shaped incision” is used, since it allows better elimination of the excess skin remaining in the middle.

Breast reduction with internal fixation

In order to give the breast more stability and increase the duration of the tightening effect with a reduction in the breast, various techniques are used in which one or more lobules from the deep skin layer (corium) and / or adipose tissue and the breast’s own glandular tissue are formed.

These lobules are attached to the shell of the breast muscle fiber or to a strong fibrous layer of adipose tissue. It is possible to perform a simulation of the shape of the breast.

Lobules of fatty tissue or glands act then as an autologous implant and improve the shape of the breast. The purpose of this type of breast reduction with the use of fixation is to remove the main breast load from the skin and skin seam, which makes it possible to perform suturing of the wound with a low level of tension and prevent or delay the repeated loosening of the skin of the breast.

Removal of body fat and breast reduction

Removing fat deposits is not an effective method for reducing breast in women. The reasons for this are obvious. By removing fat deposits, it is possible to reduce the weight of the breast, but not the skin surface area.

The result will be considerable flabbiness of the skin of the breast. Still, the removal of fat deposits combined with a reduction in breast size can be of great help. A large breast often has a wide base.

It has a process that reaches the axilla. This process can be corrected during the first operation only by means of a very long incision in the pectoral fold.

In this situation, the aid will be provided by the removal of fat deposits by means of which the outgrowth can be sucked either during the first operation or during a repeated operation (6-12 months) in order to reduce the volume without lengthening the incision.

Complex nipple = nipple + nipple circule

During breast reduction and breast lift, the nipple complex undergoes changes. It decreases and moves higher. With a not too significant upward movement, the Filatov stalk is used, which provides blood supply to the nipple circule. If it is too long, broken or pinched, the blood supply can be significantly hampered.

The nipple becomes pale (insufficient blood flow) or blue (insufficient blood outflow). In case the blood supply is not restored, necrosis of the part of the nipple complex is possible.

This leads to a prolongation of the healing process, the duration of which depends on the volume of dead tissue. However, this phenomenon is very rare today, even with a significant reduction in breast size.

In exceptional cases, if the nipple needs to be lifted by more than 10 cm, in a consultation conversation should discuss the possibility of moving the nipple complex. This means that the nipple is completely separate and sew in a new place. In the first 5-7 days for such a transplant for the entire thickness of the skin, fixation with pressure is urgently needed so that it can grow. The success of the operation can be judged only after 1-2 weeks. Currently, transplantation of the nipple complexes is carried out only in exceptional cases.
