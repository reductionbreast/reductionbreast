Breast reduction (mammoplasty) in South Africa is an operation that at first glance is less common than a surgical enlargement of the mammary glands.

However, if the installation of implants carries only an aesthetic task, then the reduction in size also has a functional side: a large breast can cause discomfort associated with sports and clothing.

Sometimes indications to reduction mammoplasty are problems with the spine, which, in turn, lead to blood circulation disorders, neurological functional disorders, difficulty breathing.

Obviously, such symptoms significantly reduce the quality of life, so in this case, breast reduction can be considered not only as a way to improve the aesthetics of the body, but also a justified requirement for the normalization of health.

Usually the reason that breast size becomes its owner’s problem becomes:

  • Genetic predisposition;
  • Birth and breastfeeding of a child;
  • Strong weight gain;
  • Hormonal disorders in the body.

It is known that up to ten percent of the female population in the world faces such problems, therefore reduction mammoplasty is a very demanded procedure, and sometimes it is carried out according to medical indications.

Features of the procedure

After the correction, the breasts not only decrease in size, but also take a beautiful shape, it becomes easier. The tissues become dense, which makes the body look younger.

The effect of the operation can last indefinitely, if there is no factor of repeated sharp stretching of the skin due to lactation or weight gain followed by a sharp slimming.

Before And After Male Breast Reduction In South Africa

Before planning an operation, you need to make sure that there are no contraindications to health.

Among the pathologies that prevent surgical operation, include:

  • Mental disorders;
  • Viral infections at the time of surgery;
  • Age to 18 years;
  • Any somatic diseases beyond remission, including oncology, diabetes mellitus and pathologies associated with blood coagulability.
  • Removal of breast tissue;
  • Removal of excess skin;
  • Plastic surgery of the nipple associated with its movement and a decrease in diameter.

The planning of the operation must necessarily take into account the menstrual cycle of the patient, because the carrying out of surgical interventions on menstruation days is fraught with bleeding.

Preparing and carrying out the operation

Preparation for reduction mammoplasty in South Africa includes a number of laboratory tests, an electrocardiogram and ultrasound examination of the mammary glands.

When carrying out the removal of breast tissue, the surgeon excludes the zones located in the lower and lateral parts of the chest, which results in an anatomical form without signs of medical intervention.

Thus, you can achieve a beautiful shape and reduce the size of the mammary glands.

The maximum size of the mammary glands removed during surgery can reach one kilogram. The peculiarity of the choice of the place for the incisions is that even before the scar resolution, they are practically not visible.

Recovery

Recovery period

The patient can leave the clinic in the absence of complications can already within a day after the operation. Dressings and medical examination are carried out daily for one week in an outpatient setting.

Suture material used during the reduction mammoplasty, does not require removal and self-absorbed within two weeks, after which the woman is able to take a shower and their own to cope with household chores.

Scars after mammoplasty take a mild form only a few months after surgery. If after six months the traces of surgical intervention remain visible, cosmetic correction can be carried out under local anesthesia.

After the operation, it is important to observe the prescription of the doctor for at least two months: to exclude physical activity and lifting weights, then the healing process will pass quickly and without the slightest complications. It is necessary to protect the breast from injuries: shocks, which must be taken into account when choosing ways of leisure.
