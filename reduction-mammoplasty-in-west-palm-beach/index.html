Indications for breast reduction surgery

Breast augmentation can cause a number of factors: heredity, weight fluctuations, pregnancy, the intake of certain medicines, in particular, contraceptives, and the age that provokes the accumulation of adipose tissue around the breast and between its lobules.

In addition, the breast can genetically have a large size and not very successful shape. The reasons that lead to breast reduction surgery can be represented by two teams.

Medical indications: very large breasts increase the risk of developing cancerous tumors and, moreover, significantly hamper research, for example, such as mammography. The volumetric mammary gland interferes with sports, can cause painful sensations, make it difficult to breast-feed.

Aesthetic indications: if the breast is disproportionately large and, moreover, has no shape, it does not look very nice. Many women, and men, consider the formless breasts ugly, which causes psychological discomfort, violates personal and even professional relationships.

Anatomy

Since the anatomically female breast is primarily a glandular tissue, it is not possible to radically affect its size with the help of diets or physical exercises. The maximum that women seek, resorting to the above measures, is depletion of the fatty tissue of the gland, which leads to an insignificant decrease in its size and loss of its final form.

Dietary and physical methods work well in the age group, when the increase in the volume of the breast is caused by a common weight gain. At this age, you can somewhat reduce the breast, resorting to liposuction. In other cases, reducing the breast and giving it a beautiful form is the competence of a plastic surgeon.

Preparatory stage

At the consultation, the plastic surgeon asks many questions, to which the patient should respond with all frankness. In particular, any discomfort she is experiencing with the current breast size, if there is currently a complaint, whether in the nature of cancer and so on. It is also necessary to tell the doctor about the presence of chronic diseases, relapsing conditions, in particular, fluctuations in blood pressure, about systemically taken medications.

The plastic surgeon, for its part, informs the patient about the nuances of the operation, the applied anesthesia and recovery rules, the time of healing of the seams, etc., forming a realistic expectations for the woman.

Important!

By the time of breast reduction surgery, a woman should have a stable weight of at least 3 months.

Before surgery

Two weeks before the breast reduction surgery, you need:

Quit smoking, since smoking worsens the course of the postoperative period, inhibits healing of the wound and several times increases the risk of complications;
Stop taking all medications that affect blood clotting – aspirin, some contraceptives and psychotropic drugs.
And also in advance to purchase a special supporting bra, which is worn after suturing the wound and applying an aseptic bandage.

Operation

On the eve of the appointed day, already in the ward, pictures of the chest “full face” and “profile” are taken, the doctor examines the patient and marks the lines along which incisions will be made. Anesthesia is used, as a rule, general or combined.

Technique of operation

Optimal access to the gland tissues is chosen by a plastic surgeon. The most popular are the periareolar incisions, along diameter of the areola and then vertically downward, and also from the margin of the areola to the submammary fold and slightly to the sides.

The volume of tissue to be removed is calculated very carefully, since the remaining should be sufficient to perform a full cover function. After resection of excess, the surgeon proceeds to restore the breast and harmonize the nipple-areola complex with a new size and shape according to a special method. In the end, cosmetic sutures are superimposed, thin drains can be left in the wound.
