Breast Reduction Before And After In Canada

Look at photos of works of plastic surgeons of Canada. Plastic surgery for breast reduction, or reduction mammoplasty, is aimed at reducing the volume of mammary glands in women surgically.

The relevance of this type of intervention is due to the fact that with excessive breast size, women feel severe pain in the back, neck, and shoulders.

The popularity of breast reduction in a particular category of women is due to the excessive burden of too heavy mammary glands. Also, patients suffer from maceration and diaper rash in the area of the pectoral fold.

All this significantly changes a woman’s way of life, leading to its passivity, as well as the oppression of the psychological state associated with feelings of inferiority.

Thus, reduction mammoplasty has a smaller bias towards aesthetic medicine and for the most part is a measure for the improvement for women with macromastia.

Stages of preparation for surgery

If a woman has an indication for surgery, first of all, she needs to pass the appropriate tests. The operation is an invasive procedure, so it is mandatory to undergo a laboratory blood test, and for women over 35 also need a mammogram or ultrasound of the mammary glands. Do not use all kinds of vitamins, non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs 14 days before surgery. It also requires the refusal of smoking. If there is inflammation in the operated area, it must be eliminated.

How is it done and what is obtained

Currently, many methods of reduction mammoplasty have been successfully developed. One of the most common and optimal is the way in which macromastia is cured by forming a T-shaped inverted incision with the subsequent formation of a similar scar in the postoperative period. Together with the scar with this technique, the lower leg is also formed.

This variant of the operation is preferable in that it is possible to restore an excellent aesthetics of the breast shape with the possibility of a maximum reduction in the volume of the gland. The peculiarities of such intervention are that the scars are located in the area of the pectoral fold, are cosmetically invisible, and a year later they disappear altogether.

The result of the operation is that the patient’s breast becomes significantly smaller by several dimensions. This significantly improves overall health and psychological condition of the woman.
