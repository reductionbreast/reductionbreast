Breast Reduction Cosmetic Surgery In Columbia, SC

Patients decide on breast reduction surgery in Columbia because they want to alleviate physical symptoms caused by excessively large breasts.

Typically, such an operation can help to solve these problems, since it improves the size and shape of your breasts – it will be more proportional to your body, and clothes will sit better.

You need a breast reduction procedure if:

– Your breasts are too large compared to other body proportions;
– You have a heavy sagging breast, while the nipples look down;
– One breast is much larger than the other;
– You have pain in the back, neck and shoulders (as a consequence of the large size of the breast);

– You have irritation on the skin under the breast;
– Bra straps leave traces on the shoulders;
– You have limited physical activity;
– You are unhappy with your breasts or feel insecure about yourself because of the large size.

Surgery to reduce mammary glands can be performed at any age, but plastic surgeons recommend waiting until the breast stops its development. Childbirth and breast-feeding may have significant and unintended consequences that will affect the size and shape of your breasts. However, many women decide to go for breast reduction even before they give birth to children, because they think that in the future they will be able to take action and eliminate all unwanted changes.

If you plan on breastfeeding, you should discuss this with your plastic surgeon.

During the consultation you will be asked about the desired size of the breast and will ask other questions about your wishes. This will help your surgeon understand your expectations and determine if they can be achieved.

Techniques for breast reduction procedure in Columbia

There are many variants of surgical approaches. Such factors as the size and shape of your breasts, as well as the size of a reduced breast will help your plastic surgeon determine the best technique for you.

In some cases, it is possible to avoid horizontal incision under the breast or vertical, which is located from the bottom of the areola down to the skin fold. If the breast is extremely large, the nipples and areola are separated from the main tissue, and then they rise upward. In this case, most likely you will lose sensitivity and the possibility of breast-feeding.

Preparation for surgery

Depending on the age, and history of breast cancer in your family, the surgeon may recommend a mammogram before the operation and a mammography examination several months after the operation. This will help to identify any subsequent changes in the mammary gland tissue. The procedure for breast reduction does not increase the risk of breast cancer.

If you smoke, you need to quit smoking before the operation. Aspirin and anti-inflammatory drugs can cause increased bleeding. Therefore, you should avoid taking these medications before surgery. Your surgeon will give you additional pre-operative prescriptions.

The procedure for reducing the mammary glands is usually performed on an outpatient basis. But you need to stay in the hospital for one night. When you are discharged the day after the surgery, make sure that someone brings you home and preferably stayed with you, at least on the first night.

Day of operation

The main task of your plastic surgeon and his assistants is to make this event (the so-called experience of plastic surgery) as easy as possible for you.
Breast reduction surgery can be performed in the hospital. Typically, this procedure uses general anesthesia. When the operation is completed, you will be placed in the intensive care unit, where you will be under close supervision. You will have drainage tubes to avoid accumulation of fluid in the chest. Your breast will be bandaged with gauze bandages, and a bandage or surgical bra will be worn.

How will I feel and look like after surgery?

Dr. Gregory Sexton Breast Reduction Surgery Procedure Candidate Image Result

The next day after the operation, you will be allowed to get out of bed for a short time. In a few days you can already move around. It is necessary to avoid stress, any kind of flexion and raising hands, as this can cause swelling or even bleeding. You must sleep on your back to avoid pressure on the chest.

All drainage tubes will be removed in a day or two after the operation, while the dressing will be replaced or removed altogether. After the operation, you will need to wear a supporting bra until the swelling disappears. A week after the surgery, the stitches will gradually be removed (for about three weeks).

You may notice that sensitivity has decreased in the area of the nipples and areola. Usually this is a temporary phenomenon. However, it may take weeks, months or even more than a year for sensitivity to recover. It takes some time for the breast to become natural. The incisions will initially be reddish or pink and will remain so for many months after the operation.

After the procedure for breast reduction, you can return to work (depending on the occupation) in a few weeks. In most cases, you can resume most of your actions (moderate activity) after a few weeks. You will still experience some discomfort, which is quite normal. If you have severe pain, tell your doctor.

For at least one week, you must refrain from sexual contact. However, your plastic surgeon may recommend a longer abstinence. For the next six weeks, you need to be very gentle with your breasts.
