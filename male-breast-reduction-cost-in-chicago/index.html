Causes of Male Breast Enlargement

The condition of male breast enlargement is not one to be commonly talked about; however there is a wide range of factors that can cause this condition to occur.

The factors range from various hormonal imbalances, tumours, congenital disorders as well as those which cannot be determined (idiopathic).

Some of the more common causes are listed below.

Gynecomastia

The term of gynecomastia is used in reference to the pronounced appearance of breasts in the male.

There is no direct cause associated with gynecomastia however it’s believed that an imbalance of hormones is the indirect basis for this ailment.

Both males and females have the ability to develop breasts at birth as they have a gland and fat component when born, while it is the hormones that will stimulate the growth of the breasts this combined with weight gain will increase the fat content of the breast resulting in their growth.

Dr John Y.S. Kim, MD, FACS, Chicago Plastic Surgeon Gynecomastia

Gynecomastia may be treated with hormone therapy or surgery or potentially a combination of both, you should speak to your physician to determine the correct treatment suited to your condition.

HYPOGONADISM

Hypogonadism is caused by decreased androgen or the bodies increased resistance to androgen which can be caused by various external factors like chemotherapy, mumps, torsion or infection or may be a factor of low testicular function in which case it is referred to as hypogonadotropic hypogonadism.

If Surgery Is the Answer

If your physician has determined that your condition is best treated with surgery as it has stabilised and will not cause continued breast growth selecting a clinic for your procedure is your next step.
