Excessive breast (Gynecomastia) is a benign condition. There is no medical reason to correct it.

However, men who have gynecomastia may seek treatment due to the negative psychological impact.

Most of these men do not like going to the beach or the gym and are very self-conscious of their body.

If you are in this category of men, you might benefit from treatment. Surgery for Gynecomastia is usually done as outpatient surgery.

Removing Excess Breast Tissue for a More Masculine Chest

Excessive breast tissue in a male is a condition known as gynecomastia. This condition can affect both young boys and adult males alike and is often unkindly referred to as “moobs”. Although it is a condition that does not pose any medical threat, the condition often leaves boys and men feeling self conscious about their appearance.

Because the issue is with breast tissue and not just fat, male breast reduction surgery is the only way to correct the condition. No amount of diet or exercise will be able to reduce the size of the breast.

When considering any type of cosmetic surgery, it is always important to do it for you and not for anyone else.

If you are concerned about the size of your breasts, then you may benefit from reduction surgery. The condition, gynecomastia, is quite common and underlying causes are rarely found. An evaluation by your plastic surgeon can help you decide whether you would be a good candidate for the procedure.

Male breast reduction surgery increasingly popular

Male breast reduction surgery, or the correction of gynecomastia, is an extremely popular surgical procedure. Patients appreciate the more toned and athletic appearance after the surgery. In our practice, the priority is to establish a good pectoral contour and to minimize scars much as possible.

The majority’s cases can be performed through 3 mm incisions and using ultrasound liposuction. In our practice, we commonly used digital software to help morph the patient’s photographs so that they can get an idea of the results they can expect.
