Indications for breast reduction

  1. Disproportionately large breasts (cosmetic indications).
  2. Large breasts that cause physical discomfort or physiological disorders of the body (physiological indications).

Common to all methods of performing breast reduction surgery is to perform incision in the lower semicircle or around the areola with its extension in the vertical direction from the areola to the crease under the breast. After excision of excess skin, subcutaneously fatty tissue and, if necessary, part of breast tissue, newly formed are fixed in the prescribed position.

There are little visible (hidden in the pigmented area areolas) and visible vertical scars, and when excising a large excess of tissue complexes – scars in the form of an anchor.

ANESTHESIA

General anesthesia is used: intravenous or endotracheal anesthesia.

Breast reduction surgery lasts 2-3 hours, depending on the amount of surgical intervention.

Hospital stay. Considering the use of general anesthesia during the operation, as well as for the purpose of controlling hemostasis, we recommend that you stay in the hospital 24 hours.

Postoperative period and recovery.

Within a few days, there are possible painful sensations caused by the surgical intervention itself. Social activity with certain physical limitations is restored 5-7 days after the operation. Complete physiological recovery occurs in a month.

The result of the operation can be estimated after 5-6 months.

Risks

The modern technique of surgical intervention, the qualification of the surgeon and the level of equipment of the clinic make it possible to reduce possible risks to a minimum.
