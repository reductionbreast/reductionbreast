Breast Reduction Prices In Texas

Plastic surgery for breast reduction is most often performed in order to relieve physical discomfort (dyspnea, back pain, inflammation in the crease under the breast) and facilitate physical activity. The operation is performed by a plastic surgeon under general anesthesia and takes an average of 2 hours.

After the operation, the patients remain in the clinic for 2 days, the length of stay depends on the patient’s postoperative state. Within 1 month after the operation, you must wear a special bra.

After the breast reduc the breast, there are always scars: on the crease under the breast, around the areola and vertically – from the areola to the crease under the breast. The final result of such an operation can be estimated only after about one year.

With breast reduction, the glandular tissue of the breast, fat tissue and skin are removed. When carrying out the reduction surgery, an incisions is made around the border of the nupple circle. Then, the nipple circle should be moved upwards.

In this case, there are many described techniques and directions of the incisions. Without the subsequent formation of scars, the operation is impossible! You can only try to keep the least number of scars, trying to make them as invisible as possible. However, the desire to reduce the number of scars also has its limits. The main principles of breast reduction will be described later.

The direction of the incision breast reduction

Various methods of breast reduction have been described in the literature. There are no significant differences between them.

The most important techniques can be divided into those performed by means of a T-shaped incision, a vertical incision and an L-shaped incision, they are performed in the same way as with a breast lift.

Technique of breast reduction with the help of a T-shaped incision.

The technique of breast reduction with the help of a T-shaped incision is uniquely one of the most frequently used techniques.

The scar that appears at the end passes around the nupple circle, then vertically to the pectoral fold, and in this fold in the transverse direction, as an inverted “T”.

With this technique of breast reduction, surgeon can remove only a lot of skin in both the horizontal and vertical directions. Due to this, it is possible to expect a good final result without fixation and without folds even in the presence of a large and / or sagging breast.

The final result is achieved quickly enough, as there is no need to wait for a long contraction of scars and skin. However, this technique involves the need for long stitches. For this reason, if possible, it is advisable not to use this technique for women prone to the formation of thickened scars (hypertrophic scars, keloids).
