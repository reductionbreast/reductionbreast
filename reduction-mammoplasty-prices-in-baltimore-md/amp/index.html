Breast reduction, also known as reduction mammoplasty, allows to remove excess fat from the breast, as well as glandular tissue and skin. This allows you to achieve a breast size proportional to the body, and to alleviate the discomfort associated with excessively large breasts.

What is breast reduction surgery?

Breast reduction surgery in Baltimore is done in a hospital or surgical center, usually using general anesthesia. The operation usually takes 3 to 5 hours.

Sometimes the operation can be performed with local anesthesia. To remove tissue and skin from the chest, the surgeon makes one or more incisions, eliminates all unnecessary and sutures the breast.

Breast reduction surgery in Baltimore is performed to change the size, weight, elasticity and shape of the breast. You can decide to undergo a breast reduction surgery to:

Risks

The most common risks of breast reduction surgery include:

  1. Scars. Unevenly located nipples or breasts of slightly different size or shape.
  2. Loss of sensation in the nipples or breast. This is often temporary, lasting several months, but for some women this lasts much longer or becomes permanent.
  3. Inability to breastfeed after surgery. Some women may not be able to breast-feed, depending on the type of operation.
  4. Less common is damage to the blood supply of the breast during surgery. This can delay the healing process of the skin.

Other surgical risks include:

  • Excessive bleeding during surgery.
  • Infection.
  • Reaction to anesthesia.
  • Blood clots in the veins and pulmonary embolism.
  • These risks can be serious or even life-threatening, but they are rare.
