In us, it is inherent in nature that the mammary glands react very sensitively to any changes in the hormonal background. The breast is a target organ for the action of hormones: prolactin, somatotropic hormone, placental hormones and indirectly – the hormones of other endocrine glands.

In addition to estrogens and progesterone for the development of mammary glands during puberty, their function in the reproductive period and during pregnancy, involution (reverse development) in postmenopause have a regulating effect of about 15 hormones.

Hypertrophy of the mammary glands or hypermastia or gigantomastia or macromastia is a pathology in which there is a significant increase in the mass and volume of the breast (and, as a consequence, the deterioration of its shape), due to uncontrolled proliferation of glandular and adipose tissue. This condition is more typical for girls in the puberty period and women of reproductive age. With macromastia occurring in the active reproductive period of a woman’s life (outside pregnancy and puberty), the growth of the breast is not as impetuous nature.

The growth of the mammary glands in girls begins at puberty, after which, generally, breast enlargement stops. However, during the life of the breast may increase and / or decrease, resulting in degradation in shape. Among the reasons leading to these changes, the main ones are:

Technique of breast reduction

Reducing the volume and improving the shape of the mammary glands is a standard operation, creating an excellent result only in the hands of an experienced plastic surgeon.

For this purpose, a surgical operation is performed – reduction mammoplasty, which allows “to kill two birds with one stone”: on the one hand, to eliminate health problems, solve medical problems, and on the other correct the volume and shape, thereby giving the breast a natural and aesthetic appearance.

Currently, more than 100 varieties of operations for reducing the volume of the mammary glands are known, their differences are in the methods of tissue removal, application of incisions and the final form of postoperative scars.

The plastic surgeon chooses the technique of breast reduction each time individually, depending on the characteristics of the structure, the degree of enlargement and ptosis of the mammary glands. In this article we give the most common techniques.

BREAST REDUCTION: OPERATION TECHNIQUE

The procedure for breast reduction surgery and the type of access (location of the incisions) is determined at the time of the surgeon’s examination.

The choice of the doctor is influenced by the initial volume of the breast, the degree of its ptosis, the presence of asymmetry, the form of areolas and other anatomical features of the patient.

Breast reduction is always performed under general anesthesia. The duration of the operation depends on all the above parameters and can take from 2.5 to 6 hours.

During surgery, the surgeon removes excess tissue, and the rest of the gland “collects” and moves in such a way as to form a beautifully shaped breast – it is at this stage that all operations are different.

Reduction Mammoplasty Is Carried Out Under General Anesthesia Through An Inverted T Incision

During breast reduction in Birmingham, the congenital or acquired asymmetry of the mammary glands is also corrected and the areola is reduced.

It is important to understand that with breast reduction without scarring can not do. As a rule, they are identical to the location of postoperative scars during mastopexy (breast lift) and relative reduction mammoplasty and at this stage all operations are similar and are divided into only two types.

Types of incisions and scars after surgery

All incisions are stitched bioresorbable threads, that do not need to be removed – so-called seamless operation.

Vertical Reduction mammoplasty

When performing vertical reduction mamoplasty, the incisions and then the postoperative scars are located around the areola and vertically: from the lower margin of the areola to the pectoral fold. This technique allows you to create a beautiful conical shape, to ensure the stability of the result and reduce the number of “folds” and reduce the load on the seams, so they heal almost without a trace.

There Will Probably Be Some Moderate Discomfort After Surgery

T-shape or Anchor mammoplasty

At very high initial size mammary grands horizontal component must be added in the projection submammary furrow. In this case, the postoperative scar will resemble the inverted letter T (inverted T-shaped or “anchor” scar). All incisions are covered with cosmetic sutures, modern self-resorbable threads. Therefore, after the operation, traces remain on the skin, which will heal very quickly, and after a few months, will take the form of thin lines with a width of 0.5-1 mm.

BREAST REDUCTION: LEUZHUR’S METHODOLOGY (M. Lejour, 1990)

Vertical reduction mammoplasty according to Lejour assumes the execution of circular incisions around the areolas and vertical ones, which are directed downwards, to the submammary fold. This technique is effective when a small breast reduction (from 200 to 500 grams) is required and can not be used in the case of gigantomastia.

This method is the least traumatic for the tissues of the mammary glands. Therefore, the risk of complications in the postoperative period is minimized. In addition, this method is remarkable in that it guarantees the absence of scars in the submammary fold.

Dr Michael S. Beckenstein Massive Breast Reduction Surgery

This methodology provides maximum stability result, quick operation, but it will be executed an additional incision in the crease of the breast: incision is made around the areola, vertically downward and submammary sulcus line. Thus, the incisions resemble the shape of the anchor, which gave the name to the method. With its help, the reduction of large breasts is effectively performed, the tissues of the lower slope are removed by a single block. It is performed with the displacement of the nipple-areolar complex in a natural position and with the isolation of the vascular pedicle, which preserves the blood supply of the nipple-areolar complex.

BREAST REDUCTION: THE HAMMOND METHOD (D. Hammond, 1999)

This is an innovative method in the field of reduction mammoplasty. The method of correction of SPAIR (Short-scar Periareolar-Inferior pedicle Reduction) allows to reduce the breast by several sizes and the formation of a short only vertical scar. After surgical intervention in this technique on the breast remains a trace in the form of a comma directed to the region of the axillary cavity. This technique gives remarkable results in the form of an attractive breast shape with a natural fullness of the upper and lower slope.
