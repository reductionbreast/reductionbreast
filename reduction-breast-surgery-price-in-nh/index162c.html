Reduction Breast Surgery Price In New Hampshire

There are several methods of breast reduction in New Hampshire. The most important difference between the methods lies in the origin of blood for circulation through the areola of the nipple.

The method is chosen individually at the doctor’s appointment. During the operation, the areola area moves to a new location. Surgical scars are usually around the nipples and vertically down from them.

When removing a very large number of tissues, one has to resort to an incision of a curved shape, from which a scar remains under the mammary gland.

Breast reduction is performed under general anesthesia. The operation lasts 3-4 hours. After the operation, the patient remains in the ward for the night, and she returns home the next morning with her accompanying person.

Post-operative care

Usually, surgical wounds do not cause pain, but it is still recommended to use pain medication regularly for 2-3 days after the operation.

After the operation, until the stitches are removed, the breast is constantly supported by a soft bra.

When the wounds heal, you can use a regular bra. Physical stress and bath visit should be avoided until the seams (8 days) are removed. The recovery after the operation takes about 3 weeks.

You can start sports two weeks later. To minimize scarring, we recommend using a Mepiform patch until their redness disappears. On average, this requires 8 months (3-12 months).

Possible problems

Breast reduction surgery in New Hampshire can be associated with common complications, such as bleeding and inflammation of operating wounds. However, they are rare.

During the operation, they try to keep blood circulation in the areoles and their sensitivity, but this is not always easy to do.

If the sensitivity is disturbed, it usually recovers within 3 months, but in a few cases, a decrease in sensitivity may remain.

Violation of blood circulation delays healing and may lead to the need for small additional measures. As a rule, they do not worsen the final result. Breast reduction may impair the ability to breastfeed.

Various studies have shown that this occurs in about 50 percent of cases. Due to the prevalence of breast cancer, a mammogram is usually performed before the operation on the mammary gland, and, if necessary, microscopic (histological) tissue samples are taken during the operation.

Scar formation is very individual, and in some patients scars can spread regardless of the method of surgery and post-operative care.
