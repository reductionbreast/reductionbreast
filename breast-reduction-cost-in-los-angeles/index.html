Breast Reduction Cost In Los Angeles

Surgery to reduce the breasts is one of the most complex operations in plastic surgery.

It is performed not only for aesthetic, but in some cases, for medical reasons.

Excessive large mammary glands negative impact on the general health of the woman.

Breast reduction In Los Angeles is necessary, because apart from psychological discomfort, too large breast, can cause a number of problems: deformities of the spine, chronic mastopathy and other problems. A woman systematically experiences discomfort.

Types of reduction mammoplasty:

  • Reduction of the mammary gland by removing excess skin and moving the nipple-areolar complex.
  • Reduction of the mammary gland by resection (partial removal) of the internal tissue, with preservation of the nipple-areolar complex.
  • Reduction of the mammary gland by completely removing internal tissue (with gigantomastia).

Preparation for surgery

Breast reduction surgery is carried out only after a careful special examination (general somatic, as well as the correction zone itself) and development of the most relevant and safe individual algorithm for solving the problem.

Be sure to plan for the preoperative examination should include consultation of the oncologist and breast physician and mammography.

Anesthesia

Reduction mammoplasty in Los Angeles is performed under general anesthesia. The operation lasts up to 3 hours

The task of reduction mammoplasty is reduced to a decrease in the size of the mammary gland by resection (partial removal) of the glandular tissue, and also to an improvement in their shape.

Basic principles of breast reduction surgery:

  1. Breast reduction by removing excess skin and internal tissues. (Internal tissues may not be removed, removed partially or completely).
  2. The movement of the nipple-areolar complex, as often there is concomitant ptosis of the mammary gland.
  3. Preservation of nourishment and innervation of the nipple-areolar complex.
  4. Fixation of the gland tissues to the anterior abdominal wall.
  5. Breast reduction for young patients should be carried out in such a way that the possibility of lactation is preserved.
  6. It is necessary to remember about the possible blood loss when removing a large volume of the mammary gland (about 1000 g).

Postoperative period

Drainage remains in the postoperative wound for a day. Intradermal sutures applied at the end of the operation dissolve independently. Some of the seams are superimposed on the areola. They are removed on the 14th day.
