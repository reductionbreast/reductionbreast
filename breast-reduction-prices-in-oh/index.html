Breast Reduction Surgery in Ohio – What to Expect

We have compiled some information to help you understand what happens before, during, and after breast reduction surgery (reduction mammaplasty) in OH. This information is general in nature and is not a substitute for instructions from your plastic surgeon. We have broken this section of our site down into three categories: pre-op, the surgery itself, and post-operative.

Pre-Op:

– Your plastic surgeon should discuss with you what can reasonably expect once the procedure is done and what you can do to help facilitate your desired results.

  • Your plastic surgeon will consider your hip size when helping you determine a new size for your breasts, to provide you with a balanced overall look and to prevent a pear-shaped appearance.
  • He or she may require a mammogram.
  • If you are a smoker, you may be requested to stop smoking 2-3 weeks prior to the operation to aid in wound healing.
  • You may also be required to stop taking products containing aspirin to help prevent excessive bleeding during surgery.
  • You may also be required to temporarily discontinue birth control or other medications containing hormones.
  • Your plastic surgeon may use a permanent marker to mark where the cuts are to be made, since the breasts do not maintain their normal shape when the patient is lying on her back.
  • You should also fully understand what types of complications can occur and the risks involved with the procedure.
  • Most often, you will be required to fast after midnight the night before your surgery. This is standard advice for anyone undergoing anesthesia.

During Your Breast Reduction Surgery

Reduction mammaplasty is almost always performed while the patient is under general anesthesia. The patient is asleep throughout the procedure. The surgery itself takes from two to four hours.

You will probably have to report to the surgical facility ahead of time so that you can prepare for anesthesia. Depending on the arrangements that are made for your surgery, you may have to arrive at the facility an hour or more beforehand.

There are a number of different breast reduction techniques that your plastic surgeon may employ. During surgery, your plastic surgeon will remove the excess breast tissue and move your areola and nipple into their new positions. After the excess tissue is removed, the skin is adjusted to reshape the breast and minimize the appearance of scars.

Breast Reduction Canton Ohio

Usually, the patient’s nipple will remain attached to its blood vessels and nerves.

However, if the breasts were very large before surgery, the nipples and areola may have to be removed and placed into a new position. This grafting, if needed, will usually result in a loss of nipple sensation.

During surgery, some tissue may be sent for biopsy to confirm that the patient’s breast tissue is normal.

After surgery, the patient is moved to the recovery area and monitored while the anesthesia wears off.

Post-Op: Recovery After Breast Reduction Surgery in Ohio

Which technique your plastic surgeon uses will affect when you can resume normal activity, how your scars will heal, and when your breasts will settle into their new shape. Your breasts will be swollen and bruised after surgery, but the pain is usually classified as moderate, and can be controlled with pain relievers. Many women can resume work within 2 weeks of surgery. Most patients are told to avoid strenuous exercise or sexual intercourse for about 3 weeks after surgery so their scars can heal properly. Your doctor will review your post-op care with you, including:

Breast Reduction In Columbus Ohio

  • What type of bra you should wear (night and day).
  • How to care for your scars.
  • Whether or not to ice your breasts.
  • When to change your dressings.
  • When you can resume normal bathing (oftentimes, doctors will suggest sponge baths post-op).
  • When you can resume physical activity.

It is important to note that scars as a result of reduction mammaplasty will fade after 6-12 months, but not disappear completely. How and when your scars will heal depends upon a variety of factors, including whether you are diabetic, a smoker, are overweight, and the elasticity and oiliness of your skin.
