How is the operation performed?

Most often, breast reduction surgery in Vancouver takes from one and a half to three and a half hours. Methods can vary; The most commonly used method involves the removal of excess breast tissue and adipose tissue in the lower part of the breast in combination with a nipple shift upward and possibly a decrease in the nipple.

In some cases, breast reduction may be accompanied by suction of fat tissue in the breast area. Especially in the case of very large breasts, this can improve the desired result.

Depending on the size of the breast, various techniques are used. During these various techniques, the scars are in different places.

What should I expect after breast reduction surgery?

During the first few days, the breast will be susceptible to hypersensitivity and swollen. Various cooling methods can help reduce swelling during the first few days.

Moderate pain in the wound area is the norm, and can be easily eliminated by taking medication. Prescribed drugs must be taken strictly according to the prescriptions. In case of sudden pain, bleeding or numbness, immediately inform your surgeon. Also, for any other outstanding issues, contact your doctor.

The level of healing will be controlled at regular intervals. Drainages will be removed within the first few days after the operation.

The stitches will be removed, as a rule, after a lapse of two weeks. A few days later, you can remove your bandages.

It is recommended, during the first 3-6 weeks after the operation, to wear a tight, protective bra, in order to reduce the swelling of the breast, and in order not to interfere with the healing process.
