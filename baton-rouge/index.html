Reduction Mammoplasty In Baton Rouge

Breast reduction or reduction mammoplasty in Baton Rouge, LA is a surgical procedure aimed at reducing disproportionately large and heavy mammary glands, improving their size and contours. Large breasts often causes muscle tension and pain in the shoulders and neck, the back, as well as skin irritations and abrasions. In addition, the large breasts are not aesthetically pleasing, can limit physical activity and cause disruption of posture.

Breast reduction surgery

Before breast reduction surgery, you need to get consultation of the breast physician. At the stage of consultation with a surgeon it is important to discuss your wishes and listen to his opinion.

Each patient, like every doctor, has a different idea of the desired size and shape of the breast.

43 Year Old Woman Treated With Breast Reduction By Doctor Lucius J. Doucet, MD, Baton Rouge Plastic Surgeon

The doctor will examine your chest and make the necessary measurements, explain the features of the operation in connection with your age, skin condition, shape and breast size. Breast reduction is not recommended for women planning to postpone pregnancy in the future.

Breast reduction surgery in Baton Rouge, LA is usually performed under anesthesia and lasts 1.5-3 hours. The surgeon removes excess fat, the tissue of the gland itself, the skin and moves the nipple and areola to a new position. Then, the surgeon compares the skin flaps around the areola, forming a new contour of the breast.

Tissues are joined by special cosmetic sutures. The sutures are usually located around the areola and vertically down to the crease under the breast. Up to a kilogram of breast tissue and adipose tissue can be removed from each breast.

After breast reduction surgery, the pain in the wound area is insignificant and quickly passes after the application of pain medications. The final result of reduction mammoplasty can be estimated after 4-6 months.

Possible complications after breast reduction

Fortunately, significant complications after the procedure of breast reduction occur infrequently. Every year thousands of women successfully undergo an operation to reduce mammary glands. However, considering mammoplasty, you need to know not only about the benefits, but also about the risks.

Possible risks and complications are best discussed in person with your plastic surgeon.

Some of the complications that need to be discussed with you include a reaction to anesthesia, bleeding, possible infections. In rare cases, during the operation, a patient may need a blood transfusion. Then, the surgeon will advise to donate your own donor blood in advance.

Breathing Problems Caused By Large Breasts

After a reduction, the breast may not be completely symmetrical, or the height of the nipples may be different. If you want later, you can make minor adjustments. Breast and nipples can lose their sensation forever, but this does not happen often. Where the incisions may have healed poorly, revision surgery may be needed (correction operation).

In the most rare cases, with damage or loss of the nipple and areola, a skin implant is used to restore them. You can help reduce certain risks if you follow the advice and instructions of your plastic surgeon.

The results of breast reduction surgery

This procedure reduces the size of the breast and makes it more elastic. Now you can enjoy sports and various active activities without experiencing any discomfort. Over time, the stitches will heal and disappear.

It is important to understand, that the lines of the incision will be visible for a long time, and how long depends on the individual characteristics of each woman. Fortunately, the incisions are easily hidden places, and they will not be visible even in dress with a deep neckline.

Breast Reduction Surgery To Help Reduce Back Strain, Skin Irritation

Appearance changes cardinally after breast reduction. Therefore, it may take some time to get used to the new outline of the body. Many women are usually satisfied with their breasts after the operation.

Strangely enough, but the largest number of patients, satisfied with the plastic surgery, were subjected to this particular procedure.

How Long Do the Results Last After Breast Reduction?

If you do not become pregnant, do not put on some weight or lose a significant amount of weight, then the size of your breast should remain constant.

However, the force of gravity and aging will ultimately change the size and shape of the breasts of almost every woman. If after years you will not like your breasts, you can make a breast lift to restore the contour and shape of the breast.

Maintaining a relationship with your plastic surgeon.

Your plastic surgeon will continue to observe and evaluate your condition. After the postoperative observation is completed, you can come to the surgeon for periodic examinations. Please remember that the relationship with your plastic surgeon does not end when you leave the hospital. If you have any questions, you are concerned about something, or you need more information, you should contact your doctor.
