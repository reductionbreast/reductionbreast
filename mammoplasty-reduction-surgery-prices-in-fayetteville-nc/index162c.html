Breast Reduction Surgery Prices In Fayetteville, NC

Breast reduction or reduction mammoplasty in Fayetteville is an operation whose purpose is to reduce the size and improve the shape of the mammary glands.

Not all owners of a large breast feel pride in their forms.

For many women, an excessively large bust causes not only physical discomfort (it is impossible to sleep on the belly, tight clothes “do not sit”), but also creates prerequisites for diseases and abnormalities: osteochondrosis of the cervical and thoracic areas, posture disorders, stretch marks and striae formation.

During the surgery, the doctor is faced with the task not only to perform breast reduction, but also to create a new, more attractive form.

Reduction mammoplasty is performed in several stages, during which the following surgical procedures are performed:

  • Excess skin is excised;
  • Removes the necessary part of the glandular and adipose tissue;
  • A complex correction of the nipple-areolar complex is carried out with its further movement into the necessary position (reposition).

There are many variations of breast reduction surgery in Fayetteville. They differ in the methods of tissue removal, the nature of the incisions and the specificity of the seams. Usually surgical access is provided through incisions of the anchor type.

They are created along the edge of the areola, stretching down to the pectoral fold and along it. The choice of a specific technique for reducing breast size is determined at the stage of preliminary consultation with the surgeon, based on the individual anatomical features of the patient.

Operation and recovery period

Doctor Saira Saini, Fayetteville Plastic Surgeon Breast Reduction Post Surgery

The procedure for reducing the mammary glands is performed under general anesthesia.

Its duration in most cases is 2-3 hours, although in particularly difficult cases, breast reduction may take five hours. The recovery period in the hospital lasts one to two days.

Within a month the patient is obliged to wear compression underwear and adhere to the regimen prescribed by the doctor. The sutures are removed on the 12th day after the operation to reduce the breast.

You can return to the usual rhythm of life after 1-2 weeks. The result of correction is not immediately apparent. A preliminary assessment of the breast reduction can be made after 1.5-2 months, and the final one in six months.

Contraindications

During reduction mammoplasty, the risk of damage to the milk ducts is very high, which calls into question the possibility of further breastfeeding. For this reason, surgeons are not advised to make breast reduction for women planning a pregnancy. In aesthetic medicine, there are alternative breast reduction techniques, such as liposuction. Early complications after breast reduction

To the problems that often arise immediately after the procedure, include: inflammatory processes, bleeding, suppuration, the emergence of hematomas. Treatment in this case is realized without surgical intervention.

Quite a rare complication is infectious inflammation, in which the patient is prescribed antibiotics. Uneven healing of various skin areas, characteristic of many post-operation cases, should not be taken as a cause for concern.

There are other complications in the reduction of breast. Usually they are the result of errors of surgeons, or the result of violation of postoperative recommendations. Rough violations of the technique of surgery can lead to discrepancies in the sutures, partial or complete necrosis of the nipple-areolar complex.

Late complications after reduction mammoplasty

Pronounced scars and their slow healing are common postoperative problems that are most often noted in smoking patients. Early rejection of a bad habit will accelerate the healing process of tissues, thereby reducing the shape and thickness of the future scar.

Traces of surgical intervention can be eliminated with the help of additional surgical correction. Partial loss of skin sensitivity is one of the possible complications with a decrease in the breast. In most medical cases, recovery of sensitivity occurs within 1-3 months.

Removal of a large volume of glandular tissue and a violation of the ducts can lead to the impossibility of breastfeeding in the future, which necessarily warn the patient. After surgical correction, there may be a slight asymmetry of the breasts. Relapse of hypertrophy is another complication prone to develop in young girls.
