Breast Reduction London Prices

Women, who are worried about too big breasts are turning to our clinic. It can be both emotional discontent, and physical discomfort and even pain. For many people, the excess weight of breast tissue interferes with an active lifestyle.

Breast reduction surgery in London, also known as reduction mammoplasty, removes excess fatty tissues of the breast, glandular tissue and skin to achieve a breast size proportional to the body.

What is the difference between breast reduction and breast lift?

Breast reduction (reduction mammoplasty) is performed more for medical reasons, when a woman has a sore back and she wants to reduce the size.

As a rule, reduction mammoplasty is done starting from D-C breast size.

Breast Size Reduction Surgery With Dr Elena Prousskaia Peregudova

A breast lift (mastopexy) can be called an aesthetic operation, when a woman wants to return a high and elastic breast, so here in most cases, a breast lift using implants is done.

Breast Reduction Surgery

Breast reduction surgery in London is usually performed through the incision on the breast and involves surgical removal of excess fat, glandular tissue and skin.

In some cases, excess fat can be removed by liposuction in combination with surgical methods. If the size of the breast is largely due to the fat tissue and excess skin tissue is not observed, then to reduce the breasts can be used liposuction. The approach used to reduce breast size will depend on your individual parameters, physique, desired volume of reduction, your personal preferences and the results of consultation with the surgeon.

Bust Reduction Surgery May Become Necessary If The Size Of The Breasts Causes Physical Problems

Stage 1 – anesthesia. Modern preparation and devices of the last generation are used for anesthesia.
Stage 2 – the incision.

Incision options include:

  • Circular incision around the nipple-areola complex;
  • Incision in the form of a keyhole or racket with incision around the areola and vertically down to the fold of the chest;
  • An incision in the form of an inverted T or an anchor-like incision.

Stage 3 – removal of tissue and its repositioning.

Moving the nipple. The nipple, which remains attached to its original source of blood vessels and innervation, moves.

Reducing the areola. If necessary, the areola is reduced by excising skin at the perimeter. Formation of a new breast. The basic tissue of the breast is compacted, pulled up and takes shape. Sometimes, in the case of very large sagging breasts, the nipple and areola, it is necessary to remove and transplant higher on the breast (transplantation of the nipple).

Chest Reduction Will Be Done Under General Anaesthetic

Stage 4 – stitching the incision. Stitches are superimposed deep into the breast tissue to create and maintain new breast forms. The incisions line remain forever, but in most cases almost disappear and become invisible over time.
Stage 5 – evaluate the result. The results of breast reduction surgery are immediately visible and you will immediately feel relieved. Over time, postoperative swelling will subside, and the incision lines will disappear.

Preparation for surgery

Preparation for surgery is better to begin not less than a week. Well, if you stop smoking and taking drugs that affect blood clotting. After consultation, you have one’s tests. Before all the tests, we determine the date of the operation. This is important, since the tests are valid for 21 days from the time they are taken.

Tests you can have in the clinic, as well as undergo ultrasound examination of the mammary glands and consult a breast physician.

On the eve of the operation, you talk with an anesthesiologist. 1-2 days before surgery, try to exclude from the diet heavy food and drink plenty of fluids.

On the day of surgery, you come to the clinic. You are accommodated in a comfortable ward.

If you are afraid of anesthesia, I can assure you that modern anesthesia is safe. We use Sevoran. The safety of this drug is indicated by the fact that it is used for children from the first days of life. And after the operation there are no headaches, nausea and vomiting.

Modern anesthetic machines of the latest generation are used for anesthesia. The operation is performed under general anesthesia and lasts 2-3 hours. As soon as the operation is over, you will immediately wake up. And already in the mind you will be transferred to the ward. Before and after the operation “the whole world revolves around you.”

Each ward is equipped with apparatus for monitoring the operation of vital organs; The oxygen supply system is connected. And most importantly, each ward is equipped as an intensive care unit. “Alarm buttons” for an emergency call of medical personnel are in the ward and in the toilets.

The emergency room doctor is in the operating room all the time, during the operation, and is on duty at the clinic 24 hours a day. After the operation, until you finally wake up, next to you is a nurse. And then, until the very discharge, 24 hours a day you are under close supervision of video cameras, the image of which is displayed on the monitor of the nurse’s post.

After discharge, we will meet again several times. First on the dressings. And then several more times during the year.

Postoperative period

After the operation, you need to take 5-7 days to take antibiotics. You can take a shower on the 2-3rd day after the operation. In the first week after the occurrence of minor pain in the area of a postoperative wound, it is sometimes necessary to take painkillers.
