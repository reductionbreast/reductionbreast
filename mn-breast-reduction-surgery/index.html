Minnesota Breast Reduction Surgery

Breast reduction surgery in Minnesota is designed to solve health problems and improve the appearance. Its main goal is to correct the breast, so that it is proportional to the structure of the body. Breast reduction surgery is especially popular among women with large breasts or sagging breasts that suffer from side effects such as back and shoulder pain, difficulty in exercising, etc.

During the operation, fat, breast tissue and breast skin are removed, and a more resilient breast of smaller size is formed. You can also reduce the size of the areola (the pigmented area around the nipple) and adapt it to the new breast size. As a result of the operation, the breast becomes smaller, lighter and more elastic, and also better suits the proportions of the woman’s body.

Reasons for surgery

Health problems such as back and neck pain, spine deformity and scoliosis, trouble with breathing, the appearance of deep lines on the shoulders from the constant pressure of the bra straps, inflammation, irritation, fungus under the breast fold, etc.

Aesthetic problems, such as breast asymmetry, breast reduction after childbirth and breastfeeding (when the skin lost its elasticity and stretched out), a large size, negatively affecting the quality and interfering with sports activities.

The best candidates for surgery in Minnesota are mature women who understand the nature of the operation and have realistic expectations about the results that can be achieved. Breast reduction is not recommended for women who intend to breastfeed.

About operation

The duration of the operation is from three to four and a half hours. There are various methods of breast reduction discussed with the surgeon before surgery. The two most common methods are:

The scar in the form of an anchor (long scar) is the most common method for reducing the large breasts, which leaves the scar in the form of an anchor. The scar is located around the areola, down from the areola to the thoracic fold and along its length.

The shape and arrangement of scars can vary depending on surgical methods. The surgeon will remove adipose tissue and breast glands along with excess skin. The size of the areola will also be reduced.

The nipple and areola rise to a new place, and the breast is attached to the shape with the help of internal seams. Usually the nipple and areola remain connected to the blood supply and nerve endings.

In rare cases, when the breast is very large and saggy, it is necessary to detach the areola and nipple from the blood supply and nerve endings and transplant them to a new place, like a skin graft. In these cases, the nipple will lose erotic sensitivity, and the woman will not be able to breast-feed.

Chest Reduction Surgery Is Performed Under General Anesthesia And Usually Takes Between Two To Four Hours To Complete

A short scar is a method suitable for small and medium sized breasts. In this procedure, an incision is made around the areola and in the direction of the crease under the breast. Since the excess of the skin are not completely removed, at the end of the operation there are sometimes small skin folds in the lower part of the chest. Usually they disappear within 3-5 months. If they do not disappear, you need to perform a small operation to remove them.

Breast reduction for men (gynecomastia)

Gynecomastia is usually performed when the breast is very prominent and represents a social and aesthetic problem for a man. These simple and relatively fast operations are performed in the department of plastic surgery in the medical center.

During surgery (which is usually performed under general anesthesia), breast tissue is removed and liposuction is performed. The recovery period after surgery is short, and the patient can return to normal activities within a few days. Over the shoulders of the department of surgery already hundreds of such operations and a rich experience in their conduct.
