BEFORE OPERATION

Before the operation, we recommend that you seek personal advice from a specialist in our clinic. Our consultant will talk about the postoperative period, as well as the possible risk and cost of the operation.

He will choose the most suitable way for you to carry out the operation. You will receive referrals for necessary medical examinations (laboratory tests, mammography or ultrasound), which are mandatory.

You will need to present the results of the tests to the doctor. Our consultant will collect and prepare data on the general state of your health, but during the consultation do not forget to tell him about all the factors that can affect your health. If the state of health is satisfactory, then we will set the day for surgery!

In the day of surgery

On the day of the operation, you will have a consultation with the operating surgeon, who will choose the method of operation that is suitable for you and make an “operating chart”. After this, an anesthesiologist will talk with you. If necessary, an additional medical examination will be carried out.

ANESTHESIA: Breast reduction surgery is performed under general anesthesia, if there are no contraindications.

The goal of mammoplasty is to reduce the mass of the mammary glands and give the breast aesthetically acceptable shape. The essence of the operation is to remove excess mammary tissue, move the nipple-areolar complex, avoiding damage to the blood vessels and nerve endings.

As with any surgery, breast reduction surgery requires incisions. The incisions makes during the operation remain around the areola, along the lower pole of the gland and the submammary fold. The incisions are sewn and fixed. The duration of the operation is 3-5 hours.

After operation

The first hours after the operation, you will be under the supervision of an anesthesiologist. If necessary, you will be given painkillers. Postoperative pain sensations are individual and removed with the help of an anesthetic drug. As a rule, the pain disappears two days after the operation. After operation possible swelling and bruises, but they usually disappear within 1-2 weeks. The first night after the operation, you will spend in the clinic under the supervision of a nurse. The next day the doctor, who performed the surgery will inspect, remove the drainage and change the bandage, after which you will wear special compression underwear (fixing bra).

This underwear helps to speed up the healing process and reduce swelling. Wearing the underwear is necessary during the whole healing period (about 3 months).

Within three weeks, you will have to refrain from physical activity. There are no contraindications to walking, but running or jumping can adversely affect your condition. It is necessary to unload the shoulder belt as much as possible.

Contraindicated are also active sports such as tennis, swimming, aerobics and so on. It is recommended to have sexual abstinence within a week after the operation, because sexual activity negatively affects the processes of healing of surgical sutures.

Smoking also has a negative effect on recovery processes. Nicotine narrows the blood vessels, including the skin, which prevents the normal flow of blood.

Postoperative care for seams

Postoperative suture should be under a sterile bandage until removal. It should be changed in case of contamination or wetting.

Sterile bandages can be bought at the pharmacy. After taking the shower, the surgical suture should be dried and then a new sterile bandage applied.

The postoperative suture should be kept dry. In case of getting wet the bandage it needs to be changed. Until the seams are removed, and also within two weeks after, the sauna and the sun are contra-indicated: both the direct rays and the solarium. In the case of severe pain in the seam area, its redness, fever, excessive bleeding, excessive discharge of fluid from the seams or the presence of an unpleasant odor, it is necessary to consult a doctor. Sutures are removed approximately 10 days after the operation.

Care for the postoperative scar

The scar is formed after the removal of the joints. You can influence the acceleration of the recovery process yourself. For clarification and softening of the scar, you can use the silicone gel Kelo-cote, which is sold in pharmacies. To avoid pigmentation, the scar should be protected from sunlight for three months.

Possible risks and complications

With any surgical intervention, there is a certain risk of postoperative complications associated with infection or reaction to anesthesia, as well as scarring. However, most complications can be easily avoided with the correct preparation for the operation and the complete set of pre- and postoperative instructions and recommendations.

In most cases, complications are minimized or completely prevented, and the patient evaluates all possible risks with the surgeon in the course of pre-operative consultations. This operation is a practically safe surgical procedure with a low percentage of pathology. Most people have no complications or lethargic, fast and almost imperceptible.
