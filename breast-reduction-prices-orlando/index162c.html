Not for every woman a large bust is the dream of a lifetime. Many owners of large breasts are dissatisfied with their size.

And for this there are weighty reasons: difficulties in choosing underwear, which would well support the breast, as well as in choosing outerwear; Sagging of the mammary glands with age; Pain in the back from the fact that “wearing” a large chest greatly strains the spine; Disturbed breathing during sleep; Intertrigo in the folds under the mammary glands.

An important role is played the appearance of the breast. Like all organs in our body, the mammary glands are subject to changes – with age they lose elasticity and shape.

They are strongly influenced by the child’s feeding, too fast weight loss and genetic predisposition.

Breast Reduction Patients Are Some Of The Happiest Patients

The problem of a large breast is that loss of shape and elasticity leads to excessive sagging, which causes psychological discomfort for a woman.

She begins to experience difficulties in intimate communication with the opposite sex.

Although, according to statistical surveys, men like women’s breasts simply as a symbol of femininity, and its size and shape for them is not so important.

Nevertheless, if the mammary glands cause inconvenience to a woman, physical or moral, it is better to contact a plastic surgeon.

Reduction of mammary glands is called reduction mammoplasty. Most often, the reasons are unsatisfactory appearance of the breast or medical indications.

There are two methods for performing reduction mammoplasty in Orlando: lifting and actually reducing the mammary glands.

These methods are similar in technique, only with the lift, the skin and some subcutaneous fat are removed, and during the reduction of the breast, it is necessary to remove also the glandular tissue.

Breast Reduction Surgery With Dr. Thomas Fiala

The concept of “big breasts” is very vague, so we can say that the lift is used to correct the large breasts, and the reduction is used to correct a very large breast.

Reduction mammoplasty in Orlando is one of the most complex operations. The surgeon should think not only about creating an aesthetic effect, but also about trying to preserve the main function of the female breast – lactation for feeding the baby.

Unfortunately, often after reduction mammoplasty, the ability to lactate is still lost, therefore, this procedure is not recommended for women who did not give birth.

The difficulty of reduction mammoplasty in Orlando is also in the fact that you have to change the location of the nipples, and the end result should look natural.

Scarless Breast Reduction Orlando

Before the operation, the patient necessarily undergoes a full examination by breast surgeon in order to identify possible pathologies of mammary glands, oncological formations or other contraindications to reduction mammoplasty.

If the tests are satisfactory, the surgeon decides on the technique of the operation. Reduction mammoplasty is performed under general anesthesia and lasts 2.5-3 hours.

If a method of breast lift has been chosen, the incision will be slightly less than with a breast reduction. The stronger the sagging of the mammary glands, the more work the doctor has to do, since he needs not only to reduce the size of the breast, but also to redistribute its volume correctly to achieve a beautiful shape.

During the operation, some fatty tissue is removed, tissue and skin, areola and nipple are “put in place”, that is, they are transferred to a higher position, but so that it looks natural.

What To Expect During Surgery

Then absorbable sutures are applied to the tissue, and to the skin – cosmetic. The patient spends at least 2-3 days in the clinic.

In the incisions after the operation, special drainage tubes are left, through which the outflow of tissue fluid occurs, and this process should be controlled by medical personnel.

The following day the bandage is changed, so the patient can take a shower before applying new bandages.

On the fifth day drainage is usually removed, the seams are removed and put on the so-called surgical bra, supporting the mammary glands in a special position.

The main complications of mammoplasty include swelling of the mammary glands, bruises, pain sensations, a temporary decrease in the sensitivity of nipples and skin in the region of the incisions. By the end of the recovery period, most of these side effects will disappear.
