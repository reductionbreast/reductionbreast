Breast Reduction Cost In Louisville KY

Just like too small breasts, and too large breasts can cause anxiety, feelings of inferiority, but also be a source of various health problems.

Plastic surgery solves this problem through breast reduction surgery in Louisville.

Preparation for breast reduction

The first step to a successful operation is a detailed consultation with the surgeon. It is necessary that the doctor who will perform the operation understand how the woman represents the future size of her breast.

In the case of an extremely large chest, the surgeon will ask questions about those health problems that can arise as a result of the large weight of the breast.

Before the operation, you need to have a preoperative examination, along with this it is necessary to undergo a mammological examination. The surgeon should be informed of all the diseases, that concern the woman, about the medicines that you takes, if necessary about your allergic reaction to certain medications.

During the operation, the patient should not be ill with any acute infectious disease, including the common cold. If the patient is taking medication, the doctor will probably advise them to stop taking them for a certain time before the operation.

It is also necessary to inform the surgeon if the woman has a predisposition to excessive scar tissue. In this case, the doctor probably does not recommend performing the operation.

Progress of the operation

Breast reduction in Louisville is performed under general anesthesia. Intervention lasts usually 2 to 3 hours. Depending on the amount of intervention, it can last longer.

The surgeon during the intervention lifts the nipple-areola several centimeters up. After that, he removes part of the breast and excess skin. The incision passes near the areola of the breast and the subsequent scar has the shape of an anchor.

Breast care after surgery

Immediately after surgery, the sites undergoing surgery are painful with swelling and bruising. Usually after 1 – 2 days a woman leaves to continue treatment at home. And there it is necessary to maintain the resting mode for several days.

Instead of bandages, you will need to wear a special bra for 6 to 8 weeks. If the absorbable stitches were not used, you will need to remove the stitches after 15-20 days. The sick leave is usually given for 7 to 10 days. If a woman performs hard physical work, but she should be at rest for a longer time.

Within a few weeks after the operation, the swelling will subside, and the final result can be considered the condition that will come in 3-6 months. It is important to go for regular check-ups to the surgeon and do not forget about regular breast examinations as a prophylaxis of malignant breast diseases. Scars on the chest can not be exposed to sunlight for six months.

Breast reduction and breastfeeding

The ability to breastfeed after breast reduction depends on the method of surgery used and the size of the remaining part of the breast. It is also important to keep the nipple sensitive, in order for reflexive formation and milk production to take place.

Despite the fact, that plastic surgeons are very careful at the time of operations on your breast, trying to save a woman’s ability to breastfeed, some of them are recommended to do breast reduction surgery after the woman does not plan the pregnancy.

Complications of breast reduction surgery

Just like after each surgery and after breast reduction there is a danger of postoperative complications.

This, for example, bleeding, infection in the wound or healing with the formation of excessive scarring.

In case of soreness, redness, hardening or increase in temperature required to be operated on contact with the doctor who will take timely measures against complications.

As a specific complication, an incorrect placement of one or both nipples may occur. In the case of a major reduction – the asymmetry of the breast or the irregular shape of the breast.

In some cases, surgical correction of such conditions will be required. In rare cases, the areola or nipple may die, as well as the formation of cysts. A number of complications of this nature can be avoided by correctly selecting an experienced surgeon.
