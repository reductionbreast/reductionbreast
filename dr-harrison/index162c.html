Is there a difference in the look/esthetic of a drainless tummy tuck versus one with drains?
Apr 30, 2019
I don't believe there is a discernible difference in your post op appearance whether you use drains or not.  The bigger difference is in my patients recovery.  Since I have gone "drainless", my patient's have a smoother recover and dramatically fewer seromas or post op fluid collections. Best wishes.

Can I just start with implants then get a lift later?
Augmentation vs augpexy
Feb 27, 2019
You can certainly stage your breast surgeries.  Many women shrink away from anterior breast scars and prefer to see how their breasts will appear with implants alone. Avoid the notion that a larger implant will look better by filling up your breast. I am afraid it too often results in just large too full breasts.  Visit with your surgeon and the mastopexy delay may be a good option for you.  Best wishes 
What is the best thing to do to prep for pre op & post op for lift/implant exchange surgery?
Preop regiments
Feb 27, 2019
In our office we ask our breast implant patients to do a few things preop to help in their recovery:  push high protein diet for two weeks preop, apply bactroban ointment in their nares, navel and ear canals twice daily for three days preop, shower with hibiclens starting three days preop and use Sage towelettes to coat their skin the night before and morning of the surgery, have realistic idea of what to expect from their procedure.  Best wishes

What is the maximum breast implant for me and my breast lift can be only around the nipple scar?
What is the maximum breast implant for me and my breast lift can be only around the nipple scar?
Jan 8, 2019
There is no specific maximum volume implant with a periareolar augmentation mastopexy.  I have my augmentation patients try various sizers in a sizing bra during their consultation to establish the range of volume they hope to achieve.  That determines a starting point.  We determine the exact volume implant intraoperatively, visualizing your breasts in an upright posture with sizers in your breasts.  There is a volume which will make a patient look disproportionate or heavy.  Hope that help...
