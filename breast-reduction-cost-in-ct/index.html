Breast Reduction Cost In Connecticut

Beautiful breasts are the dream of every woman. Big breasts can be beautiful and attract the attention of the opposite sex, but can be a cause of discomfort, physical and emotional.

Big breasts do not allow you to lead an active lifestyle and often cause problems with the selection of underwear, and is the cause of stoop, which affects the spine.

Many women decide to reduce their breasts not only to become more attractive, but also to simply improve their well-being.

Why Do Women Want Breast Reduction Surgery?

Breast reduction with the help of plastic surgery in Connecticut can solve problems without harm to the health of women.

This operation can be combined with the operation to correct the nipple and areola. In some cases, to achieve the optimal aesthetic result, a breast lift is also performed.

What problems can be solved with breast reduction surgery?

First of all reduction mammoplasty is carried out for medical reasons – a very large and dangling breast, negatively affecting the posture and spine of a woman.

Such a breach in the formation of the breast (gigantomastia) causes a lot of unpleasant consequences: the woman constantly suffers from headaches, pains in the neck and shoulders, circulatory disorders and respiratory processes.

Sometimes to breast reduction in Connecticut is resorted after pregnancy and feeding of the child, after which the large breast starts to sag. If you only plan pregnancy and breastfeeding, you can not perform breast reduction surgery.

How is reduction mammoplasty performed?

Breast reduction is an operation to remove excess fat, glandular tissue and skin to achieve a breast size proportional to your body and reduce the discomfort associated with excessively large breasts.

After the removal of the tissues, the skin around the areola is reduced and a new contour and shape of the breast are formed. If necessary, additional liposuction of the breast and areas adjacent to it is performed.

Breast Reduction Healing By Dr. Michael Suzman

In some cases, excess fat can be removed with liposuction in combination with excision methods. If the breast size is largely associated with excess fat tissue and there is no skin excess factor, only liposuction can be used for the breast reduction procedure.

The incisions used in a breast reduction and how to minimize scars.

Around the areola

The incisions in the form of a “keyhole” or “racket” around the areola and vertically down to the inframammary fold. Inverted T or anchor-shaped incision around the areola and vertically down to the inframammary fold and horizontally along the inframammary fold.

Surgical access does not exceed 4-6 cm. In most cases, the incision lines become invisible over time and usually they always remain hidden under the underwear or swimsuit.

Indications and contraindications for breast reduction

  • Loss of shape and sagging breasts
  • Gigantomastia
  • Unaesthetic appearance
  • Loss of breast shape after pregnancy and breast-feeding
  • Problems with posture and spine.

Contraindications

  • The presence of an infectious disease
  • Presence of oncological diseases
  • Violation of blood clotting
  • Acute diseases of internal organs, diabetes, smoking
  • Pregnancy, breast-feeding.

Before the operation:

  • Inform your doctor if any changes in your health condition occurred on the eve of surgery (runny nose, pustular rashes, etc.).
  • Do not eat or drink for 8 hours before surgery.
  • Leave valuables at home.
  • Remove make-up.
  • Take only the items of personal hygiene.
  • After discharge from the hospital, it is advisable that you are accompanied.

The operation is performed under general anesthesia, with the control of the depth of sleep and the level of anesthesia. Incisions are made on the marking and the breast is given a new form. The suturing of the wound is made, the seams are applied deep into the breast tissue to create and support newly formed breasts, an intradermal cosmetic suture is applied. In some cases, drainage is installed. At the end of the operation, a compression bra is worn.

Recovery

After the operation, the patient is usually in the hospital under supervision for 2 days. On the second day after the operation, laboratory blood tests are performed, bandaging is performed, drains are removed (if they were installed) and after that the patient is discharged home.

The patient calls to the doctor the first three days of staying at home and reports the condition health. 7 days after the operation, the patient comes to the examination to her surgeon, the bandages are removed from the seams.

Later the patient comes in a planned order for examination in 2 weeks, in a month, in 3 months, in 6 months, in a year. For 6-8 weeks the patient wears a compression bra. During the month, it is worthwhile to refrain from physical exertion, and to limit physical activity in the shoulder girdle for 2-3 months.
