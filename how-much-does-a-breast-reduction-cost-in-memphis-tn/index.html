The causes of excessive breast enlargement

The beginning of the process is the moment of puberty of the woman and in the future the hypertrophy of the mammary glands continues.

At some point, the enlargement of the mammary glands should stop, but for reasons that are not clear, the enlargement of the mammary glands continues. As a rule, pregnancy increases mammary hypertrophy, and also leads to ptosis of the breast and changes in its shape.

Factors influencing breast hypertrophy

  • Changes in the hormonal background of a woman during puberty and an “excessive response” to an increase in the level of female sex hormones of the mammary glands.
  • Hereditary predisposition.
  • Overweight.
  • Pregnancy.

Tasks of reduction mammoplasty

Reducing the weight and volume of mammary glands. Reduction mammoplasty is performed to significantly reduce breast, sometimes more than twice.

Breast reduction by 25-30% often does not give a satisfactory result. In addition, a small breast reduction does not stop the process of further hypertrophy.

Moving up the nipples and areola

The process of enlarging the mammary glands is accompanied by stretching of the skin and ligamentous apparatus of the breast, which in turn leads to a displacement of the nipple-areolar complex down, sometimes considerably lower than the submammary fold (fold under the mammary gland).

One of the most difficult tasks in the process of breast reduction is the movement of the nipple-areola complex up, to a new place.

Making the breast aesthetically appealing shape

A plastic surgeon must reduce the breast in such a way that it becomes beautiful. This is a difficult task, but it is in this and shows the skill of the surgeon.

Technique of operation

The vertical method of reduction mammoplasty implies the presence of the incision around the areola and incision down to the submammary fold. This method is suitable for a small breast reduction in the range of 200-500 g.

Its advantages are low traumaticity, absence of a scar in the submammary fold, and a low risk of postoperative complications. However, it is not suitable for reducing large breast size.

An anchoring method of reduction mammoplasty involves an incision around the areola, a vertical incision downward and in a submasmal fold.

Male Breast Reduction In Memphis TN

This method of breast reduction in Memphis allows to achieve good results with a very large volume of the breast. In some cases, the mass of tissue removed is more than 2-3 kg.

With a large breast size, ptosis of the mammary glands is inevitable, which means that the nipple-areolar complex is displaced downwards. One of the tasks that the surgeon performs during the operation is the movement of the nipple-areola complex to the starting position.

To do this, the surgeon allocates a vascular pedicle with which the nutrition of the areola is carried out, and moves the nipple-areolar complex upward.

Nutrition with the vascular pedicle of the nipple-areola complex should be sufficient, so that the process of tissue healing proceeds without complications.

Possible complications after surgery

Reduction Mammoplasty May Be Performed For Cosmetic Or Reconstructive Reasons

  • Suppuration and bleeding are complications, the occurrence of which is possible in any operation.
  • Complete or partial necrosis of the nipple-areolar complex. There is such a complication is rare.
  • Rough scars formed after surgery. The quality of scars is extremely difficult to predict. The reasons for rough formation of scars are many. Careful completion of all stages of the operation almost always guarantees a good quality of scars.
  • Dissatisfaction with the aesthetic result. It is extremely rare, if the shape and volume of the breast after the operation does not meet the patient’s expectations at all.

The postoperative period is, as a rule, without significant painful sensations. It is recommended to limit physical activity, do not raise your hands high, to avoid unnecessary tension of the seams.
