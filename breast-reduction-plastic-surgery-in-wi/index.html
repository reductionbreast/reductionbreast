Breast Reduction Cost In Wisconsin

Breast reduction surgery in Wisconsin has become more and more popular in recent years. In the US alone, thousand of women undergo this plastic operation every year and more and more surgeons are turning to this field as it’s a lucrative business.

The results which this kind of surgery may vary according to the doctor who performs the operation, your body’s healing ability, and other factors.

There is no doubt that a good surgeon can work wonders. However, what the brochures of plastic surgery clinics show is not the complete picture.

There are many potential risks involved in this sort of procedure. You need to know about them in order to be able to make the best decision for you.

Breast Reduction By Liposuction With Dr. Clifford King, Wisconsin Plastic Surgeon

Here are some of risks of breast reduction surgery:

  • Mismatching breasts. Sometimes the reduction isn’t even. This depends greatly on the skill of the surgeon but it does happen.
  • Permanent loss of feeling in the nipples or entire breast area. Temporary loss of sensation is normal.
  • Inability to breast feed. This happens because the milk ducts which lead to the nipple often need to be removed during surgery. This is a major issue to consider if you’re planning on having more children.
  • A long healing period. During this time, the breasts may be quite sore to the touch and cause you great discomfort.
  • Scars. Every breast reduction surgery involves scarring to a certain degree. Some women scar more than others. It’s difficult to know in advance how your scars will look.

In some cases there is loss of blood supply to the nipples which may cause you to lose them. Further plastic surgery and skin grafts will be necessary to improve your appearance afterward

Breast Reduction Surgery Can Interfere With Certain Diagnostic Procedures

Knowing the risks

Every surgery has risks. There is no escaping it. In rare cases you can also suffer infection and further complications.

The key is to understand the process in advance and only choose to have surgery if you feel comfortable with the risks involved.

The best way to reduce the chances of any of these risks becoming a reality is to choose your breast reduction surgeon carefully. The skill and experience of your surgeon determines a great deal.

Do not feel ashamed to ask questions. It’s your body we’re talking about here, so you need to have all the data you need.
