Cost Of Breast Reduction In Georgia

What is breast reduction surgery?

Breast reduction in Georgia is an operation aimed at removing excess skin, fat and tissue in order to reduce the size of the breast. During breast reduction surgery the surgeon makes an incision around the nipple, the nipple to the lower part of the chest and across the bottom of the chest.

After removal of fat and skin, the nipple is often moved upwards. Women with large breasts (macromastia), breast reduction is performed to reduce pain, improve self-esteem and self-confidence, improve physical abilities. Breast reduction surgery can also be performed in men in the case of gynecomastia (abnormal breast augmentation in men). Another name for breast reduction surgery is reduction mammoplasty.

What surgeons perform breast reduction surgery?

A surgeon performing a breast reduction is usually a plastic surgeon, but general surgeons can also do this.

How common is breast reduction surgery?

The operation of breast reduction is carried out quite often. According to the statistics of the American Society of Plastic Surgeons, in 2016, 90 000 women underwent a reduction mammoplasty operation in the United States.

Duration of procedure / operation:

Breast reduction surgery usually takes 2 to 5 hours.

Length of stay in hospital:

After breast reduction, the patient is stay for one night at the hospital or clinic where the operation was performed. At some operations of reduction of a breast it is required stay in a hospital within 2-4 days.

Anesthesia:

Typically, breast reduction surgery is performed under general anesthesia. If local anesthesia is used in your case, you will also need to take the medication to relax the muscles.

Recovery:

  • After the breast reduction surgery, the patient will need to take a vacation for at least a week for rest. Some patients need two weeks.

    • 33 Year Old Woman Treated With Breast Reduction By Dr John Paletta, MD, Savannah Plastic Surgeon

  • You can exercise after 4-6 weeks.
  • Swelling and bruising will begin to decline within a few days.
  • Approximately two weeks after the operation, you will need to visit the doctor again to remove the seams and bandages.

Risks:

Often occurring risks and complications in performing a breast reduction operation:

  • Reducing the sensitivity of the nipple and the adjacent area after surgery.
  • Problems with breastfeeding after the operation.

In the event that during the operation, the patient’s nipples are separated from the milk ducts, the patient may lose the ability to breast-feed. If you are planning to start a family, it is recommended the breast reduction surgery performed after the birth of the child in order to avoid these problems.

  • Scars formation. After a breast reduction surgery, permanent scars remain. In this case, after a year after the operation, the scars become less noticeable, and most surgeons make incisions so that they are subsequently visible as little as possible.

    • Breast Fat, Tissue, And Skin May Be Removed From The Breasts

  • an asymmetric position of the nipples and the unequal size of the mammary glands.
  • Unwanted presence of excess skin on the side of the chest, for the removal of which you may need one more operation.
  • Adverse reactions to medications or anesthesia.
  • Bleeding inside the breast (hematoma) with the appearance of tumors.
  • In rare cases, this is a loss of the nipple.

Recovery:

  • After surgery, the breast is protected by a supporting bandage, supporting bra or elastic bandages.
  • A week after the surgery, the patient starts wearing a soft bra and loose clothing for several weeks.
  • Drainages can be installed to remove liquids from the chest, which will be removed after three to five days.
  • It is necessary to take prescribed painkillers and anti-emetics. The doctor can also prescribe antibiotics to prevent the development of infection.

  • Do not lift heavy objects, as this may lead to an increase in the scars.
