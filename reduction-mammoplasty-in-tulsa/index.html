Reduction Mammoplasty in Tulsa

A big breast does not always bring joy to its possessor. Sometimes excessively large mammary glands can cause many problems.

Reduction mammoplasty (breast reduction) in Tulsa is a correction of the volume of the mammary glands due to the tightening and removal of excess skin, glandular and adipose tissue.

The increased proliferation of breast tissue can occur both in adulthood and in very young. Hypertrophy of the mammary glands can be caused by the following reasons:

– heredity;
– Hormonal disorders;
– Individual developmental deviations;
– obesity;
– pregnancy.

Too large breast gives a woman a lot of physical and psychological discomfort, can cause problems with the spine. There are difficulties with the choice of underwear and clothing, besides, the large breast strongly hangs, there are diaper rash.

Breast reduction in Tulsa OK is designed to save the woman from all these problems. If necessary, the operation is carried out in conjunction with mastopexy – a breast lift.

Breast reduction surgery: stages

Primary examination reveals the degree of hypertrophy, the level of ptosis, the doctor estimates the proportions of the body and discusses with the patient the planned result.

The necessary analyzes are carried out, the date of the operation is assigned. Before surgery, breast skin is marked by this marking will be made incisions – usually at the bottom of the chest.

The standard incision with a large number of operated tissues is around the areola and down to the fold under the breast.

The operation to reduce the breast is laborious enough, and therefore it is carried out under general anesthesia.

A plastic surgeon removes excess fat, glandular tissue and skin for several hours. If necessary, the size of the areola is reduced.

After the excess tissues are removed, the remaining ones are fixed by the seams in the desired position, an elastic bandage is applied. It is recommended to spend a day in a hospital under the supervision of doctors.

Recovery after surgery takes, as a rule, several months. During the month, it is necessary to wear compression underwear and avoid physical exertion, sunbathing (in the sun and in the solarium) and sauna visits. For some time, there will be obvious postoperative soreness and swelling, but in most patients it passes quickly.

Unfortunately, after the breast reduction surgery, visible “anchor” scars remain. However, with proper care for them and when carrying out cosmetic procedures, scars will become less noticeable with time. Often there is a decrease in the sensitivity of the nipples. Another important consequence: the ability to lactate can be lost.

Contraindications to breast reduction

Reduction mammoplasty is not performed in the following cases:

  • During pregnancy;
  • With exacerbation of chronic diseases;
  • With diabetes mellitus;

Possible side effects and nonspecific complications:

1. The development of bleeding is a rare phenomenon, which, as a rule, does not pose a danger and does not require additional intervention. If there is a leakage of bloody liquid from the drainage tubes during the first postoperative day, this should not cause concern, since it is normal for this stage.

2. Accession of infection, inflammation, suppuration. These side effects can be triggered by non-compliance with the patient’s postoperative recommendations, neglecting preventive antibiotic therapy.

Very rarely – in violation of the technique of performing the operation. If the surgeon has sufficient experience, this risk is extremely small.

3. Hematomas. If this is a small bruise, then they disappear on their own. If the hematoma is of considerable size, the intervention of the physician is necessary: the elimination of the source of hemorrhage requires the removal of sutures and careful washing of the wound. After repeated suturing, drainage tubes are left in it.

Complications specific

This type of side effects is related to the amount of removed material: the greater the proportion of the gland removed, the higher the risk of complications.

If the breast was very large, or the operation was performed for elderly patient, or a woman smokes a lot or has cardiovascular diseases, endocrine organs or other chronic diseases, then the most serious complication is the development of tissue necrosis in the operated area.

In this case, necrosis often threatens the elements of the nipple-areola complex or skin grafts that make up the edges of the wound.

To reduce the risk of this complication is only possible by an experienced surgeon, who carefully verifies every millimeter when applying preoperative markup and strictly follows all stages of the selected operating technique. If a significant part of the breast is removed, it can affect the sensitivity in the nipple and areola area, as well as in the adjacent skin area. But patients, who note a decrease in the sensitivity of this area, assure that they do not experience any inconvenience.

Divergence of sutures is extremely rare and requires the imposition of special ointment dressings. If the scar is more rough than expected, then it is possible to resort to corrective intervention to excise the scar. There is a risk of losing the opportunity to breastfeed in future after breast reduction. But the majority of progressive methods keep the gland ducts intact during the operation. The smaller the site of the gland has been removed, the lower the risk of damage to the ducts.

Special notes

Breasts may have some asymmetry after surgery. If the difference is insignificant, then this does not represent a problem and does not require additional measures. Normally, most women have asymmetrical breasts. In rare cases, a slight deformation of the areola is detected after the operation. This is not important, but is, in most cases, the result of inaccurate planning of the operation.
