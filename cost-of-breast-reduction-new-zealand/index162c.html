Reduction mammoplasty in New Zealand is a plastic surgery aimed at reducing the volume and improving the shape of the mammary glands.

In terms of its characteristics and achievable final result, reduction mammoplasty refers to surgical interventions aimed at preserving the health of women, in contrast to breast augmentation and breast lift operations, where the main purpose is the aesthetic component.

Indications for reduction mammoplasty:

  • Mammary gland hypertrophy, gigantomastia.
  • pronounced asymmetry of the breasts.
  • ptosis of the mammary glands.
  • Significant weight loss (reduction of the volume of the mammary glands with the existing excess of skin).
  • structural reconstruction of the mammary glands (post-lactation involution, fibrous-fat involution in the period of extinction of the reproductive function).

Contraindications to reduction mammoplasty:

  • mastopathy (diffuse, nodular), fibroadenomas of the mammary glands.

Details of the reduction mammoplasty:

Reduction mammoplasty is one of the most complex operations in plastic surgery. Surgery is performed in a hospital under general anesthesia (inhalation anesthesia). The duration of the operation is from 2 to 4 hours.

Depending on the existing baseline conditions (bust size and planned volume reduction, pronounced asymmetry, degree of ptosis, significant excess of skin, chest width, excess body weight, etc.), a specific technique for performing the operation and the location of postoperative scars is determined.

In most cases, a thin postoperative scar runs along the circumference of the areola and vertically sinks down the center of the lower pole of the mammary gland to the submammary fold. If there are significant volumes of mammary glands, an additional transverse incision in the submammary fold may be necessary – the scar will resemble the inverted “T”.

Recovery period after reduction mammoplasty:

  • In a hospital, the patient is between 1 and 3 days. Sutures are usually removed for 10-14 days. A mandatory condition is the wearing of compression underwear for 1-1.5 months. In the first few days, the patient feels moderate swelling and pain, which is aggravated by movement. During the first two weeks is strictly contraindicated sharp arm movements, especially lifting them up.
  • Most patients return to their daily activities after 2 weeks.
  • It is recommended to limit any physical activity for a period of 1.5-2 months after surgery. For a similar period it is better to refrain from visiting the sauna and solarium.
  • The preliminary result of reduction mammoplasty is evaluated after 3 months, the final result – in 1 year (after the completion of the formation of scars).
